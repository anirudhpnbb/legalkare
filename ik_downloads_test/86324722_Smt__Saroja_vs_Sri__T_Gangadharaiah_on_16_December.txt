The case of the plaintiff is that, she is a home maker and also doing voluntary services in many social organization. The defendant is known to her and her family members. The defendant is a police official and is used to borrow money from the plaintiff whenever he was in need of money and returned it at later date. The plaintiff had played a vital role in the negotiation of defendant's daughter's marriage. The marriage of the defendant's daughter was fixed on 29.11.20009. The defendant had requested the plaintiff to arrange a loan of Rs.50,00,000/- for performing his daughter's marriage and promised to clear the said loan within 6 months by disposing of the landed property of him situated at Dabaspet. The plaintiff being a person of helping nature, based on the assurance of the defendant, had arranged and lent him a sum of Rs.35,00,000/- out of her savings and borrowing from others. The defendant had executed an on demand promissory note and consideration receipt in favour of the plaintiff for a sum of Rs.35,00,000/- and also given a cheque signed in blank to the plaintiff as security. Apart from that the plaintiff has also bid her chit for 3 OS.No.6373/2012 Rs.2,00,000/- in the month of November, 2009 subscribed with M/s Kapil Chits Pvt. Ltd., and gave the same to the defendant and also the plaintiff stood as a guarantor along with her husband for the chit of Rs.3,00,000/- prized by the defendant in September, 2009. The defendant had also pledged some of his jewels from the plaintiff to arrange money for his daughter's wedding. And thereafter the defendant had not kept up his promise of repayment and when the plaintiff approached and insisted the defendant for return of the loan, his wife had abused the plaintiff in filthy words and made false allegations that the plaintiff is having extra marital affair with her husband. When the plaintiff was informed about the above deplorable act of his wife, the defendant instead of reprimanding his wife had suggested the plaintiff to divorce her husband and joint him and further the defendant had threatened the plaintiff not to insist for repayment of the above loan otherwise he will fix the plaintiff in false cases and thereafter the plaintiff had sent a legal notice dated 28.12.2010 to the defendant to refrain himself and his wife from making unnecessary allegations and also demanded to return the above said loan amount. On receipt of the notice the defendant had made frequent threatening calls to the plaintiff and on 2.1.2011, the defendant had 4 OS.No.6373/2012 entered into the house of the plaintiff and abused her in a filthy language and threatened to do away with her life and apprehending danger to her life, the plaintiff had given a complaint to the Commissioner of Police, Bengaluru seeking action against the defendant and his wife and sent copies of the same to various authorities on 3.1.2011. The Commissioner of Police had directed the jurisdictional police i.e., Rajajinagar Police to enquire and take action against the defendant and his wife. As the defendant is a serving police official, the police have not at all taken any stringent action against him and hence the plaintiff had filed a PCR.No.1662/2011 against the defendant and his wife before the 9th ACMM, Bengaluru on 12.1.2011 for the offences punishable under section 420 , 452 , 499 , 500 , 506 and 354 of IPC. 3. The further case of the plaintiff is that the defendant with an intention to defame the plaintiff had indulged in character assassination by preparing morphed photographs, wherein the plaintiff was shown in obscene manner and threatened to put it in circulation in the event of plaintiff pursuing the above said PCR. In this regard the plaintiff had given a complaint to the Rajajinagar Police 5 OS.No.6373/2012 station who in turn have registered a case in their Cr.No.22/2011 on 22.1.2011 against the defendant. The plaintiff had also filed another PCR.No.2654/2011 against the defendant for the offences punishable under 499 and 500 of IPC for defaming her. After receipt of the summons in the said case, the defendant had contacted the plaintiff and apologized for his misdeeds and sought time to repay the above loan amount stating that he did not get the potential buyers to purchase his landed property at Dabaspet and thereafter in spite of several requests and promise the defendant has neither repaid the above said loan amount nor even paid the nominal interest as promised by him and hence the plaintiff is constrained to file the present suit for recovery of the above said loan amount along with agreed interest. 4. On this point in a ruling of AIR 2005 Madras 90 i.e., in the case of Natarajan Vs. The presumption is one of law and there under the court shall presume that the instrument was endorsed for consideration". 15.
