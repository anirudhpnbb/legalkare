The present revision is directed against the order dated 30.04.2012 C.R.No. 81/12 Page 1 of 7 pages 2 passed by the court of Ld MM in CC no. 251/09 Titled Gurmeet Narang Vs. Jyoti Narang and others. The complainant had examined himself as CW­1, Sh. Sunil Kumar LDC RR­Sessions (Tis Hazari Courts) as CW­2 and Sh. Jyotish Topno Ahlmad from the court of Sh. Sanjay Garg, Ld. ADJ/N as CW­3. The complainant filed the complaint u/s 200 Cr.P.C . In brief, his complaint is that he got married to the respondent no. 1 on 18.04.2007 according to Hindu rights. Respondent no. 2 is the father of respondent no. 1. Respondent no. 3 is the mother. Respondent no. 4 is sister of the respondent no. 1 and respondent no. 5 is the brother­in­law of respondent no. 1. Respondent no. 6 is the person to whom the respondent no. 1 was earlier married. According to the complainant, the respondent no. 2 to 5 declared at the time of the engagement that the respondent no. 1 was a virgin girl and thus he agreed to marry her. The respondents no. 1 to 5 kept the complainant and his family members in dark by concealing material facts. The respondent no. 6 sent vulgar messages to the complainant in the month of February 2009 and it was disclosed that the respondent no. 1 was married to him on 07.05.2003. By getting married again to the complainant, the respondent no. 1 committed the offences u/s 494/495 IPC . The respondent no. 2 to 5 committed the offences u/s 499 IPC . The respondent no. 1 to 5 also committed the offences u/s 420 / 405 / 34 IPC. The respondent no. 6 committed the offences u/s 503 / 504 / 506 IPC and the respondents also committed the offences u/s 511 IPC. 2. By the impugned order, the Ld Trial court summoned the respondent no. 6 only after taking cognizance of the offence u/s 503 IPC . The C.R.No. 81/12 Page 2 of 7 pages 3 prayer to summon the respondent no. 1 to 5 was declined. Thus the present revision petition was filed. Notice of the petition was issued to respondent no. 1 to 5. Certain documents were filed by them but the same can not be looked into at the stage of revision. The court has to see whether the ld trial court erred in refusing the summoning of respondent no. 1 to 5 on the basis of the evidence before it adduced by the complainant/ revisionist at the stage of pre­summoning evidence. I have considered the arguments of ld counsel for the parties and have perused the record including the TCR. 3. The respondent no. 1 had filed a petition u/s 12 of the Hindu Marriage Act 1955 vide HMA No. 574/2003 Titled Ms. Jyoti Gera Vs. Neeraj Singh Chauhan. The said petition was filed with the allegation that Sh Neeraj Singh Chauhan forcibly married her on 07.05.2003 and that they had never lived together. The petition was allowed vide Judgment dated 24.07.2004 and the marriage between Ms Jyoti Gera and Sh. Neeraj Singh Chauhan was declared void ab initio. The revisionist and the respondent no. 1 got married on 18.04.2007. Ld counsel for the revisionist agreed that in view of the Judgment u/s 12 HM Act, the offences u/s 494 and 495 IPC will not be made out against the respondent no. 1. 4. The revisionist has claimed that offence u/s 405 IPC is also made out. Section 405 IPC defines breach of trust and there is no allegation of entrustment to constitute an offence of breach of trust. Therefore, the provisions of IPC punishing criminal breach of trust are not applicable in the present case. While alleging the other respondent no. 6 committed offences u/s 503 / 504 / 506 IPC it is generally stated C.R.No. 81/12 Page 3 of 7 pages 4 that the respondents committed offence u/s 511 IPC. Section 511 IPC deals with the punishment for attempt to commit an offence. There is no justification to mention this provision against all respondents for the acts allegedly done by respondent no. 6. The revisionist has claimed that the respondent no. 2 to 5 have committed the offence u/s 499 IPC . It is submitted that by concealment of the facts relating to previous marriage and inducing the complainant to marry respondent no. 1, he has been defamed. These allegations do not fulfill the ingredients of the offence of defamation. Now section 420 IPC remains. Section 420 IPC deals with the punishment for cheating. Cheating has been defined u/s 415 IPC . The ingredients are (1) Deception of any person, (2) (a) Fraudulently or dishonestly inducing that person (i) to deliver any property to any person; or (ii) to consent that any person shall retain any property, 2 (b) intentionally inducing that person to do or omit to do anything which he would not do or omit if he were not so deceived, and which act or omission causes or is likely to cause damage or harm to that person in body, mind, reputation or property. In the present case, it has been argued that the complainant / revisionist would not have married the respondent no. 1 had he been told about the previous marriage of the respondent no. 1. The complainant examined himself as CW­1 before the Ld. Trial court. He has stated that at the time of engagement, respondent no. 2 to 5 came to meet him with respondent no. 1 and they told that the respondent no. 1 is a virgin girl. The respondents never disclosed to the complainant that respondent no. 1 had earlier been married to respondent no. 6. After two months of the marriage, he started C.R.No. 81/12 Page 4 of 7 pages 5 receiving vulgar messages from respondent no. 6. He made enquiries and came to know about marriage certificate, photographs and the petition filed by the respondent no. 1. It is to be noted that the statement of CW­1 does not fulfill the ingredients of section 415 IPC. He has only stated that the respondent no. 2 to 5 told him that the respondent no. 1 was a virgin girl and they did not disclose the factum of earlier marriage. There is no evidence that the marriage between the respondent no. 1 and respondent no. 6 was consummated. The marriage between the respondent no. 1 and respondent no. 6 was declared void much before the marriage of the complainant with respondent no. 1. The complainant/ CW­1 presumed certain facts regarding the respondent no. 1 on receiving vulgar SMS from respondent no. 6. Ld. Counsel for the revisionist has drawn my attention to the certain observations made by the Court of Ld ASJ in the order dated 16.11.2009 while deciding the anticipatory bail application. On page no. 4 of the Order, it is mentioned that there is no satisfactory explanation as to why she did not file the petition u/s 12 HM Act till August 2003. It is held that these observations have no relevance in the present complaint. In his statement as CW­1, the complainant has not stated that he would not have married the respondent no. 1 had he been told about the previous marriage. This assertion has been made during arguments. To fulfill the ingredients of the offence of cheating as defined u/s 415 IPC it was incumbent upon the complainant to depose that he was fraudulently lead into the marriage which he would not have contracted had certain facts been disclosed to him and it was also his duty to show how that C.R.No. 81/12 Page 5 of 7 pages 6 concealment has harmed him in his body, property, mind or reputation. It was the duty of the complainant to establish the ingredients of the offences which he claims to have been committed by the respondent no. 1 to 5. It is also to be noted that the complainant did not examine any of his own family members as witness in the pre­summoning evidence. The statements made by respondent no. 1 and her family members in judicial proceedings regarding the status of the respondent no. 1 prior to her marriage with the complainant also does not have any adverse effect. It is not denied that when the respondent no. 1 got married to the complainant, she was unmarried and there is no evidence to show that her statement that she was virgin i.e non­consummation of her marriage with respondent no. 6 is false. It may also be noted that CW­ 1 deposed that after two months of the marriage he started receiving vulgar messages. CW­1/5 is a transcript of the SMSes. The complainant has himself mentioned on this document that the messages were received from 09th April 2009 to 25th July 2009. This shows that the messages were not received within two months of the marriage as deposed by him. 5. Summoning in a criminal case is a serious matter and the ld Trial court rightly refused to summon the respondent no. 1 to 5. The timing of filing of the present complainant by the revisionist also creates doubt. According to him, he started receiving messages after two months of the marriage. The marriage took place on 18.04.2007. The record of the SMS is for the period 09.04.2009 to 25.07.2009. FIR u/s 498­A/406/34 IPC was registered against the revisionist on C.R.No. 81/12 Page 6 of 7 pages 7 21.09.2009. He filed the present complaint on 26.10.2009. If he was really cheated he would have filed the complaint much earlier. Before concluding, it is observed that the Ld Trial court was not required to deal with the aspect of 156 (3) Cr.P.C in the impugned order. Once the Magistrate takes cognizance of the offences and proceeds with the examination of the witnesses of the complainant, the stage of section 156 (3) Cr.P.C is over. Therefore, while passing the impugned order, the Ld Trial court was not required to consider whether the matter could be sent to the police for investigation under section 156 (3) Cr.P.C. 6. Revision petition is accordingly dismissed. 7. Copy of this order alongwith trial court record be sent to the trial court. Revision file be consigned to record room.
