The first charge against the accused persons is u/s 120B IPC. Section 120A of IPC is reproduced hereunder:- Section 120A IPC - Definition of criminal conspiracy.--When two or more persons agree to do, or cause to be done,-- (1) an illegal act, or (2) an act which is not illegal by illegal means, such an agreement is designated a criminal conspiracy: Provided that no agreement except an agreement to commit an offence shall amount to a criminal conspiracy unless some act besides the agreement is done by one or more parties to such agreement in pursuance thereof. Explanation.--It is immaterial whether the illegal act is the ultimate object of such agreement, or is merely incidental to that object. 48. Section 120B IPC provides as under:- Section 120B IPC - Punishment of criminal conspiracy.-- (1) Whoever is a party to a criminal conspiracy to commit an offence punishable with death, 2[imprisonment for life] or rigorous imprisonment for a term of two years or upwards, shall, where no express provision is made in this Code for the punishment of such a conspiracy, be punished in the same manner as if he had abetted such offence. (2) Whoever is a party to a criminal conspiracy other than a criminal conspiracy to commit an offence punishable as aforesaid shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term not exceeding six months, or with fine or with both. FIR No.790/2015 PS Bharat Nagar Page 21 of 27 ­:: 22 ::­ 49. The conspiracies are hatched in secrecy and ordinarily there is no direct evidence. It can be inferred from the facts and circumstances. The circumstances of the present case clearly shows that the accused persons came on their bikes and asked PW-2 & 3 to leave the scooty. The pistol was also shown to threaten the complainant. As soon as the PW-2 & 3 left the scooty, one of the boys drove away the scooty. The robbery took place at about 04:40 PM and on the same day, the scooty was found abandoned in front of Adharshila Public School, Derawala Nagar which is not very far from the place of robbery. It clearly shows that the accused persons had specific information about the money kept in the diggi of the scooty. It further shows that the robbery was made in a planned manner for committing theft of money and not of scooty. The ingredients of Section 120B IPC have been duly established from the facts and circumstances proved on record. As already discussed, only the accused Rahul, Manish, Abhishek and Sonu have been proved as the persons who have committed the robbery. They are accordingly found guilty u/s 120B IPC. As already discussed, the material available on record is insufficient to bring home guilt against accused Kanhaiya u/s 120B IPC. 50. All the accused persons have further been charged u/s 395 r/w 120B FIR No.790/2015 PS Bharat Nagar Page 22 of 27 ­:: 23 ::­ IPC . It is proved on record that in order to the committing of theft, the offenders put PW-2 & 3 in fear of instant death or of instant hurt by showing a pistol. The offence of robbery as defined in Section 390 IPC has been proved. An offence of robbery becomes an offence of dacoity u/s 391 IPC when 5 or more persons conjointly commit or attempt to commit a robbery, or where the whole number of persons conjointly committing/attempting to commit robbery and persons present and aiding such commission/attempt, amounts to 5 or more. The prosecution must prove that there were 5 or more persons who had committed the robbery. The prosecution has proved the identity of only 4 persons namely accused Rahul, Manish, Sonu and Abhishek as the persons who had committed the robbery. To establish that there were 5 or more persons, the prosecution has relied upon the testimony of PW-2 who has deposed, "suddenly 6 boys on 3 motorcycles reached and they made us to stop prior to the red light. 4 boys alighted from the said motorcycles..........". 51. Since the number of persons is very crucial for constituting an offence u/s 395 IPC , the case requires a closer scrutiny. As regards the number of persons, PW-3 deposed, "2 motorcycles obstructed our way on which 4 persons were riding.....". There is a contradiction as regards the FIR No.790/2015 PS Bharat Nagar Page 23 of 27 ­:: 24 ::­ number of persons in the testimonies of PW-2 & 3. This particular contradiction has been dealt with in the earlier part of the judgment and was considered as a minor contradiction when it was argued that the entire case is false. However, this contradiction cannot be considered as a minor contradiction when the very fact of number of persons is in issue. PW-3 has not been countered/contradicted by the State by way of cross-examination as regards the number that he stated as robbers. This would imply that the prosecution did not agitate the testimony of PW-3 in this regard. The first view that can be formed from the evidence of PW-2 is that there were 6 persons. The second view that can be formed on the basis of unrebutted testimony of PW-3 is that there were only 4 persons. It is no longer res-integra that where 2 views are possible, the view which is more favorable to the accused has to be adopted. Therefore, the latter view of robbery being committed by 4 persons is acceptable. The prosecution has not proved beyond reasonable doubts that the robbery was committed by 5 or more persons. The charge u/s 395 IPC could not be proved. The charge u/s 392 IPC r/w Section 120B IPC is however proved against accused Rahul, Manish and Sonu. 52. The next charge framed against the accused persons is u/s 412 IPC which is an offence of dishonestly receiving or retaining any stolen FIR No.790/2015 PS Bharat Nagar Page 24 of 27 ­:: 25 ::­ property with the knowledge or having reasons to believe to have been transferred by the commission of dacoity. Since the offence of dacoity has not been proved on record, a conviction u/s 412 IPC cannot be recorded. Dishonestly receiving or retaining any stolen property, knowing or having reasons to believe the same to be stolen property is a minor offence u/s 411 IPC. As already discussed, the prosecution has proved the recoveries of stolen currency notes from accused Rahul, Manish, Sonu and Kanhaiya. The fact that the said currency notes were stolen property has also been proved on record. From the facts and circumstances it can safely be presumed that the accused persons knew or had the reasons to believe that the money was stolen property. The accused persons are accordingly found guilty u/s 411 IPC. 53. Accused Rahul has further been charged u/s 397 IPC which is an offence of using a pistol which is a deadly weapon at the time of the robbery. PW-2 & 3 deposed that it was accused Rahul who showed the pistol at the time of the robbery. 54. It is argued by Ld. Defence Counsel that no pistol has been recovered during the entire investigation hence the prosecution has failed to prove that the 'pistol' as mentioned by PW-2 & 3 was a 'deadly' weapon. It is FIR No.790/2015 PS Bharat Nagar Page 25 of 27 ­:: 26 ::­ argued that charge u/s 397 IPC could not be established on record. 55. In this regard, Ld. APP for the State argued that in Murlidhar Vs. State Crl. Appeal 279/2002 decision dt. 01.06.2018, the Hon'ble Delhi High Court held that the recovery of weapon is not necessary ingredient for a conviction u/s 397 IPC and the use of the same to threaten is sufficient. 56. I have considered the above submissions. To record a conviction under Section 397 IPC in the instant case, the prosecution must establish that the accused Rahul has used any 'deadly weapon'. In Rakesh Vs. State of NCT of Delhi, Cr. A No.208/2003 decision Dt. 20.07.2010, the Hon'ble Delhi High Court discussed in detail the aspect of use of a deadly weapon. It was held in unambiguous terms that if the accused is not carrying a weapon or carrying a weapon which is not in a working condition and cannot cause any grievous harm or injury, it would not qualify and cannot be regarded as a deadly weapon within the meaning of section 398 IPC. In the case in hand, since no pistol has been at all has been proved on record, the prosecution could not prove that the 'pistol', that was shown by the accused Rahul to put PW-2 & 3 under fear, was in fact a serviceable pistol. In my considered opinion, the reasoning given by the Hon'ble High Court in Rakesh Vs. State (Supra) FIR No.790/2015 PS Bharat Nagar Page 26 of 27 ­:: 27 ::­ case will squarely apply to section 397 IPC. For want of any proof that the accused Rahul used a deadly weapon at the time of the offence, the case will fall short of Section 397 IPC. Therefore, no conviction under Section 397 IPC can be recorded against the accused Rahul. 57. It is pertinent to note that u/s 222 CrPC, a conviction for a minor offence can be recorded though the accused has not been charged with it. CONCLUSION 58. The findings of the court in view of the discussion made herein above are as under:- (a) Accused Rahul, Manish and Sonu are acquitted of charges u/s 395 / 120-B & u/s. 412 IPC . They are held guilty and accordingly convicted u/s 392 / 120B IPC, u/s 120B IPC and u/s 411 IPC; (b) Accused Kanhaiya is held not guilty and acquitted of the charges u/s 395 / 120B IPC, 120B IPC and 412 IPC . Accused Kanhaiya is held guilty and accordingly convicted u/s 411 IPC; (c) Accused Rahul is acquitted of the charge u/s 397 IPC.
