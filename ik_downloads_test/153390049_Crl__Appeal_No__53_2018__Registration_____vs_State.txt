When this aspect was put to the parties in this court during hearing of the appeals, the appellant Om Prakash informed that on that date he had come to the court for depositing the cost but the Advocates were on strike and hence, the same could not be tendered. On subsequent date 04.06.2018, the accused Om Prakash had moved an exemption application on medical grounds which was not allowed and accordingly no further opportunity was granted. Be that as it may, I may observe that vide order of the Ld. Sessions Judge dated 01.03.2018 the examination of the witness R.K. Jain was subjected to the condition of payment of cost but there is nothing to show that the payment of Rs.15,000/­ was a condition precedent for summoning of the witness. I may also note that though in his oral submissions the respondent Sh. Vivek Garg submits that the witness was present on two dates having been summoned, yet the perusal of the Trial Court record confirms that neither the witness was summoned upon filing of PF nor he had appeared or was present before the Ld. Trial Court for tendering himself for cross examination. 11. This being the background, without making any observation on merits with regard to the arguments put forward by the Ld. Counsel for the appellants regarding the ingredients of Explanation­4 to Section 499 of Indian Penal Code not being fulfilled, I hereby set aside the impugned judgment / order dated 20.08.2018 and 10.09.2018 respectively, and remand the case back the Ld. Trial Court with the direction to the appellant / accused Om Prakash to tender the cost of Rs.15,000/­ to the respondent Sh. Vivek Garg before the Ld. Trial Court on the next date of hearing.
