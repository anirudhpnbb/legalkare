Rohit Karkera & vs State Of Gujarat & on 11 February, 2013 Author: S.G.Shah Bench: S.G.Shah ROHIT KARKERAV/SSTATE OF GUJARAT R/CR.MA/3048/2008 JUDGMENT IN THE HIGH COURT OF GUJARAT AT AHMEDABAD CRIMINAL MISC.APPLICATION NO. 3048 of 2008 FOR APPROVAL AND SIGNATURE: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE S.G.SHAH ================================================================ 1 Whether Reporters of Local Papers may be allowed to see the judgment ? 2 To be referred to the Reporter or not ? 3 Whether their Lordships wish to see the fair copy of the judgment ? 4 Whether this case involves a substantial question of law as to the interpretation of the constitution of India, 1950 or any order made thereunder ? 5 Whether it is to be circulated to the civil judge ? ================================================================ ROHIT KARKERA & 1....Applicant(s) Versus STATE OF GUJARAT & 1....Respondent(s) ================================================================ Appearance: MR JAYESH A KOTECHA, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1 - 2 MR RT SONGARA, ADVOCATE for the Applicant(s) No. 1 - 2 MR GAURANG K PATEL, ADVOCATE for the Respondent(s) No. 2 MR KG SHETH, ADVOCATE for the Respondent(s) No. 2 MR NJ SHAH, APP for the Respondent(s) No. 1 ================================================================ CORAM: HONOURABLE MR.JUSTICE S.G.SHAH Date : 11/02/2013 ORAL JUDGMENT The petitioners, against whom Criminal Case No. 3223/2007 has been filed by the respondent no. 2 herein before the Ld. Chief Judicial Magistrate, Jamnagar, have preferred this petition for quashing the complaint and order dated 5/5/2007 passed by 3rd Joint Civil Judge and Judicial Magistrate First Class, Jamnagar, in the aforesaid Criminal Case No. 3223/2007. Such criminal case is with reference to the allegations by the respondent no. 2 herein that both the petitioners have committed offences punishable under sections 499 , 500 , 501 and 114 of the Indian Penal Code [ IPC ] by publishing defamatory item against them on 14/4/2007 in local newspaper as well as in the news papers circulated in Saurashtra and Gujarat. 2. By an order dated 12/3/2008 this Court has granted ad-interim relief in terms of para. 5 [B] of the petition and thereby further proceedings in Criminal Case No. 3223/2007 was stayed, observing as under in para. 2. 2. It is submitted that the basis of the allegations made in the complaint is the news-paper report, Annexure-H to the petition, for which petitioners were in no manner responsible. It is submitted that none of the ingredients of the offences alleged against the petitioners are made out in the complaint. It is further pointed out that neither the Editor nor the concerned Press Reporter of the news-paper have been arraigned as accused in the complaint and that the complaint in question has been filed as a counter blast to the complaint filed by the present petitioners with a view to pressurize the petitioners to withdraw the complaint filed by them. 3 After service of notice upon the respondent no. 2, on 13/10/2008 Rule was issued with an order to continue the ad-interim relief granted earlier till further orders. 4 Heard Ld. Advocate Mr. Jayesh Kotecha for the petitioner, Mr. NJ Shah, Ld. APP for the respondent no. 1 State and Mr. K G Sheth, Ld. Advocate for the respondent no. 2. Perused the record. 5 The crux of the entire matter has been already considered by the Court on 12/3/2008 itself while staying further proceedings pending criminal case, which is requested to be quashed by this petition. The sum and substance as emerges from the record and submission by the petitioner, has been recorded by the Court in para. 2 of order dated 12/3/2008, which is reproduced hereinabove. There is no change either in the facts or circumstances and details of the case even today. However, when the matter has been finally decided, let us recollect and examine the different versions of the petitioners and respondents herein, who are accused and complainant respectively in original trial. 6 So far as the factual aspect is concerned, practically there is no dispute between the parties that respondent no. 2 complainant was working with and for petitioner no. 2 company and he was running collection agency. The petitioner no. 1 was serving as Manager of petitioner no. 2 company at the relevant time at Jamnagar. In the year 2007 when they received complaints against the respondent no. 2 and when they found some truth in such complaints, after preliminary inquiry, whereby it revealed that though the respondent no. 2 has collected huge amount from several customers for and on behalf of the petitioner no. 2, such amount was not credited or deposited with the petitioner no. 2 company and, therefore, the petitioner no. 2 company has to initiate proceedings against the borrowers. It is only during such proceedings, the petitioners have come to know about the misdeed and misappropriation of company s fund by the respondent no. 2 and, therefore, since the respondent no. 2 has committed several offences including cheating with the petitioners, they have informed the Police Inspector of City B Division, Jamnagar, on 27/2/2007 as well as on 6/3/2007 and requested to initiate investigation against the respondent no. 2. The apprehension of the petitioners has been proved because of non-action by the police since they have failed to register a complaint or to start investigation. This may happen probably because of the intimacy of the respondent no. 2 with the police since it was specifically disclosed in above two complaints to the Police Authority that practically the respondent no. 2 is threatening the petitioners by saying that nothing will happen to him even if the complaint is filed because police is within his control [police authority is in my pocket] and if the petitioners will dare to file a complaint against him, he may kill the petitioners. Some details about misappropriation is also disclosed in such complaints, but when local police has failed to initiate any investigation or proceedings against the respondent no. 2, the petitioners have no option but to prefer a private complaint before the Courts of law, which was registered as Criminal Case No. 33/2007 wherein the Court has ordered to the local police station to inquire into the complaint and to file the report, as provided under section 156 [3] of the Code of Criminal Procedure [for short Cr. P.C ]. In view of such order passed by the Ld. Magistrate on or about 12/4/2007, the Jamnagar Police Station has registered a complaint being FIR No. 7/2007 on 13/4/2007 against the respondent no. 2 under sections 406 , 408 , 420 , 504 , 506 [2] of the IPC and though there is no further information on record, it seems that the police must have started investigation at-least by calling upon the accused in this case. 7 The controversy in the present case arises from the news item published in a local newspaper, namely Ajkal on or about 14/4/2007 i.e. very next day of lodging the FIR by the police. Photo copy of such publication is at Annexure-H page 56 with the petition. Such news item discloses that the respondent no. 2, who was working as a Recovery Agent, had issued duplicate receipts and cheated the Bajaj Company, petitioner no. 2 herein, wherein at-least 30 bogus receipts were issued by him and approximately Rs.50,000/- and two motorcycles had been kept with him instead of depositing such amount with the petitioner no. 2 company. In the above news, it is also stated that for such cheating, petitioner no. 2 has filed a complaint which is being investigated by P.S.I. Of Hanuman Gate Police Chowky. Being aggrieved by the such disclosure in the newspaper, the respondent no. 2 has filed Criminal Case No. 3223/2007 on 4/5/2007 alleging that the petitioners herein have committed offences punishable under sections 499 , 500 , 501 and 114 of the IPC and, therefore, they should be tried and punished and should be sentenced to maximum imprisonment. On such complaint, impugned order to summon the present petitioners was passed on 5/5/2007. 8 In view of the above background, it is submitted by Mr. Kotecha, Ld. Advocate for the petitioners that such criminal case is nothing but a counter blast against the complaint already filed by them in April 2007. Therefore, relying upon the facts and circumstances as narrated in the petition and as supported by the documents filed on record, he is praying to quash the proceedings of Criminal Case No. 3223/2007. 9 Whereas, Ld. Advocate Mr. K G Sheth for the respondent no. 2 has vehemently argued that there is no substance in the petition filed by the petitioners because though the petitioners have disclosed about commission of offence by the respondent no. 2 on 26/2/2007 and 6/3/2007, nothing was found by the police against the respondent no. 2 and, therefore, a false complaint was filed before the Court on 12-13/4/2007, wherein the Court has passed an order to investigate it by the police. It is further submitted by Mr. Sheth that though the police had lodged FIR on 13/4/2007, based upon such complaint before the Court, immediately on the next date i.e. 14/4/2007, the petitioners have published defamatory material against the respondent no. 2 which has resulted into serious damage to his prestige in the society and it is his case that because of such publication, people has discontinued the relationship with him and other companies have also stopped to assign him the work as Recovery Agent. It is further contended By Mr. Sheth that petitioner no. 1 has managed to publish such information in the newspaper, namely Ajkal without the permission of the petitioner no. 2 company and in second breath it is stated that otherwise such permission by petitioner no. 2 is not proper. Mr. Sheth has further relied upon the decision rendered in the case of Smt. Chand Dhawan v. Jawahar Lal reported in AIR 1992 S.C. 1379 and submitted that additional documents in the form of communication and accounts which are produced at Annexures- B and C with the petition cannot be taken into consideration while deciding the petition for quashing the complaint, submitting that the order to issue summons or investigation against the complaint was based on the material which was produced with the complaint and, therefore, propriety of such order can be checked only with the relevant information which was available before the trial Court while passing the impugned order and, therefore, no reliance can be placed upon additional documents which are produced with the petition before the High Court for quashing the complaint. 10 I have heard Ld. Advocate Mr. KG Sheth at length. He has read out several passages from the complaints, both against respondent no. 2 and complaint filed by the respondent no. 2 against present petitioners as well as some paragraphs and passages from the petition itself, submitting that no case is made out to quash the complaint since the publication at Annexure-H is admitted as a defamatory by the petitioners themselves and that there is no substance in the allegations in the complaint filed by the petitioners against him and, therefore, his complaint cannot be quashed because of the injuries suffered by him, since none of the exceptions has been carved out specifically by the petitioners to prove that they are not liable for such offences. 11 I do not find any substance in the arguments of Ld. Advocate Mr. Sheth for the respondent no. 2 being original complainant of Criminal Case No. 3223/2007, wherein the trial Court has issued summons against present petitioners for alleged offences under sections 499 , 500 , 501 and 114 of the IPC. 12 The facts are very simple as narrated hereinabove i.e. the petitioners had tried to lodge a complaint against the respondent no. 2 for misdeeds, misappropriation and threats which can be confirmed in their letters/ complaints dated 27/2/2007 and 6/3/2007. It is more clear and confirmed from such complaints that the respondent no. 2 is having some nexus with the local police when he gave threats to the petitioners to kill if complaint is filed against him and further stating that the local police is in his control. Probably this fact has been proved and, therefore, petitioners have no option but to file the complaint before the Magistrate under sections 406 , 408 , 420 , 504 , 506 [2] of the IPC , which is registered as Criminal Case No. 33/07 wherein also the Court has passed order of investigation under section 156 [3] of the Cr. P.C. If any litigant has filed any complaint before the competent Court and if based upon such complaint, police registered an FIR and as it is being done in the form of FIR being No. 7/2007 dated 13/4/2007, disclosure of such information in the newspaper by local reporters as a news item can never be termed and treated as personal allegations against the respondent no. 2 by the petitioner in any form. 13 What we have to see is the nature and contents of the publication. On perusal of its copy at Annexure-H, it becomes clear that it is not in the form of advertisement or disclosure on behalf of the petitioners, but local reporter had taken the factual details for publishing such information that on presenting a complaint by the petitioner no. 1 on behalf of petitioner no. 2, the P.S.I. Of Hanuman Gate Police Chowky has started investigation for cheating by the respondent no. 2. Though such news item disclosed the manner and nature of cheating by the respondent no. 2 i.e. the respondent no. 2 has issued forged receipts to 30 customers of the petitioners and thereby collected Rs.50,000/- and kept two motorbikes with him, it cannot be said that such disclosure by the local newspaper, is in any way defamatory against the respondent no. 2 and that any offence of defamation has been committed by the petitioners. The fact is very much clear that what is published in the newspaper is nothing but factual aspect of the complaint which is lodged as an FIR by the local police station and more particularly when the petitioners have categorically stated in such complaint before the Court that the police is not taking steps against his complaints dated 13/2/2007 and 6/3/2007, they are compelled to file complaint before the Court and when such previous complaint categorically confirmed the nexus of the respondent no. 2 with the police, it transpires that, as contended in the petition, the respondent no. 2 has lodged a complaint on 4/5/2007 against the present petitioners under sections 499 , 500 , 501 and 114 of the IPC, to pressurize the petitioners either to withdraw their complaint or to compromise with the respondent no. 2. There is substance in such contention raised by the petitioners, more particularly because of the fact that even in the complaint itself the respondent no. 2 has, in para. 7, stated that the petitioners have failed to withdraw the complaint filed by them or to publish explanation in the newspaper and, therefore, there is confirmation of offence being committed by the present petitioners. 14 The outcome of the submissions advanced by both the learned advocates and record is very much clear, as it is being observed, prima-facie also, that the basis of the allegations in the present complaint is newspaper report, for which the petitioners were no-where involved, in as muchas it is not made clear by the respondent no. 2 either in the complaint or at any place that how such information has been disclosed by the petitioners to the news agency and the respondent no. 2 has failed to lodge a complaint or alleged against news agency either for the defamation or for the correctness of the information which is published as news item. It is clear that whatever is published in the newspaper is based upon the public record in the form of an FIR and complaint lodged by the present petitioners and, therefore, practically none of the ingredients of the offences as alleged against the petitioners are made out in the complaint itself. The over-all position is clear that the respondent no. 2 has filed such complaint as a counter blast to the complaint filed by the present petitioners with a view to pressurize the petitioners either to withdraw such complaint or to compromise the dispute with them, wherein the respondent no. 2 has issued forged receipts and misappropriated huge amount with two motorbikes of the petitioners. It is submitted by Mr. Sheth that in such complaint filed by the petitioners, now petitioners are not remaining present to adduce evidence. However, such statement makes it clear that at-least the complaint of the petitioner was investigated and there was prima-facie substance in such complaint wherein charge-sheet was filed and matter is pending for evidence of the complainant. In view of such fact, when news item is based upon the public record by the news agency, only because of the connection of present petitioners with such news item as an complainants, would not amount to defamation by them and, therefore, the complaint deserves to be quashed. 15 In view of the above facts and circumstances of the case, the decision rendered in the case of Smt. Chand Dhawan [supra] relied upon by Mr. Sheth is not helpful to the complainant respondent since decision to quash the complaint herein is not based upon any additional material filed by the petitioners, but only upon the factual details that present complaint is nothing but the counter blast against the complaint already filed by the petitioners and when such fact is already admitted by the respondent no. 2 in his present complaint itself. 16 Ld. Advocate Mr. Kotecha for the petitioners is relying upon decisions reported in [1] 2007 [1] H.L.H. 773, between Varshaben Devendrabhai Patel vs. State of Gujarat , [2] AIR 1977 S.C. 1489 between State of Karnataka vs. L. Muniswamy , [3] 2005 BCR [Criminal-1] 421 between Mahendra Shankarrao Godbole vs. Prabhakar Hariram Gulhane and [4] AIR 1998 S.C. 2117 between Jawaharlal Darda vs. Manoharrao Ganpatrao Kapsikar , and submits that when the complainant had set the machinery of the Criminal Court with a view to take revenge, the petition to quash the complaint is required to be allowed, High Court has inherent powers to achieve salutary public purpose and securing the ends of justice, but Court proceedings ought not to be permitted to degenerate into a weapon of harassment and it extends to set aside the pending proceedings and that when no material is available either in the complaint or in the verification to attract commission of offences under sections 500 , etc., order of issuance of process is liable to be quashed and set aside and that news item published in good faith and public good cannot be said to be defamatory. In view of the above facts, circumstances, material on record and discussion made hereinabove, it becomes clear that the complaint filed by the respondent no. 2 being Criminal Case No. 3223/2007 for the offences punishable under sections 499 , 500 , 501 and 114 of the IPC, wherein the trial Court has ordered to issue summons on 5/5/2007 is nothing but counter blast to the complaint filed by the petitioners, which is registered as C.R. No. 7/2007 and Criminal Case is pending based upon such complaint and, therefore, publication at Annexure-H as a news item by the local reporter cannot be treated as defamatory and there is no proof on record that the petitioners are in any manner connected with such publication. Therefore, the trial Court has erred in issuing summons and thereby this petition deserves to be allowed by quashing such order of issuance of summons and complaint itself by passing following order. 17 The petition is allowed. The order dated 5/5/2007 issuing summons to try the present petitioners for the offences punishable under sections 499 , 500 , 501 and 114 of the IPC in Criminal Case No. 3223/2007 passed by the Ld. Judicial Magistrate First Class, Jamnagar, and further proceedings in the said Criminal Case No. 3223/2007 are hereby quashed and set aside. Rule is made absolute. (S.G.SHAH, J.) VR Pansala.
