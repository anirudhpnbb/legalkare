Dhinesh @ Ravi 15/19 52.Before reaching at any conclusion let the relevant sections i.e. 363/367/354 IPC be re­produced, which are as under :­ Section 363 IPC:­ "Whoever kidnaps any person from India or from lawful guardianship, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to seven years, and shall also be liable to fine." Section 367 IPC : Kidnapping or abducting in order to subject person to grievous hurt, slavery, etc.­Whoever kidnaps or abducts any person in order that such person may be subjected, or may be so disposed of as to be put in danger of being subject to grievous hurt, or slavery, or to unnatural lust of any person, or knowing it to be likely that such person will be so subjected or disposed of, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to ten years, and shall also be liable to fine. Section 354 IPC "Assault or criminal force to woman with intent to outrage her modesty ­ Whoever assaults or uses criminal force to any woman, intending to outrage or knowing it to be likely that he will thereby outrage her modesty, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to two years, or with fine or with both." 53.Since the present was registered on the statement of victim. Even medical reports shows that hymen of prosecutrix was found intact and there is no redness and no external injury mark seen. As far as testimony of minor victim/prosecutrix is concerned, as a rule of prudence it has to be see with close scrutiny. 54.As per case of prosecution, there was enmity between the family of accused and SC No.37/2011 State v. Dhinesh @ Ravi 16/19 victim and due to this enmity accused had committed wrong act with victim. If we taken into consideration enmity between family of accused and victim that could not be established. PW3 Smt. Maya Devi, who is the grandmother of victim had not supported the case of prosecution on this fact and she has been turned hostile. Further, PW Amar Nath who is the eyewitness had also not supported the case of prosecution on any point of time. 55.It is a settled law that testimony of minor requires close scrutiny before reaching any conclusion and corroboration may also be required as per facts of the case. Since in the present case victim has been 07 years old and she in her examination in chief stated that in the night time accused had taken her from the chowk in a school at Nand Nagri, Delhi, by saying that he will get provide her an ice­cream but he did not give her ice­cream and accused put off her underwear, committed wrong act with her and extended threat to her. Hence, in the opinion of this court fact of offence u/s 363 IPC is attracted. In a judgment titled as 'Prakash Vs State of Haryana AIR 2004 SC 227: (2004) 1 SCC 339' it has been observed that: "The object of this section seems as much to protect the minor children from being seduced fro improper purposes as to protect the rights and privileges of guardians having the lawful charge or custody of their minor wards" 56.Hon'ble Apex Court in judgment '(1993) 2 Supreme Court Cases - State of Himachal Pradesh Vs Raghubir Singh ' where it has held that : SC No.37/2011 State v. Dhinesh @ Ravi 17/19 "There is no legal compulsion to look for corroboration of the evidence of the prosecutrix before recording an order of conviction. Evidence has to be weighed and not counted. Conviction can be recorded on the sole testimony of the prosecutrix, if her evidence inspires confidence and there is absence of circumstances which militate her veracity. In the present case the evidence of the prosecutrix is found to be reliable and trustworthy. No corroboration was required to be looked for, though enough was available on the record......" 57.In any case victim is the best witness with whom incident had happened. In the present case, from the testimony of victim it could not come on record that accused had kidnapped or abducted the victim. Further, PW Amar Nath and PW Smt. Maya Devi have been declared hostile and they had denied that victim was taken by accused to fulfill his unnatural lust. Since fact of unnatural lust could not come on record from the testimony of victim but when accused said to victim "Chalo Tumko Ice Cream Dilata Hu" is sufficient for the purpose of kidnapping and abduction and inference can be drawn that accused had enticed the victim. Moreover, MLC is silent on the fact of any wrong act or sexual act committed with victim. Since fact of unnatural lust could not come on record from the testimony of victim. Hence, section 367 IPC cannot be said to attracted. 58.Further, it his established that victim was taken by accused in the school where he put off her undergarment and had put his hand on the private part of victim. As per MLC there is no injury upon the person of victim. However, fact of outraging of modesty of victim cannot be ruled out. Hence, in the opinion of this court, fact of offence u/s 354 IPC is attracted. SC No.37/2011 State v. Dhinesh @ Ravi 18/19 59.On close scrutiny of testimony of victim, it is established that accused had taken the victim from her lawful guardianship without consent of her guardian. It is further has come on record that accused had put his hand on the private part of the victim. MLC of victim not suppose to come out with any conclusion as far as section 367 IPC is concerned it could not come on record that accused had committed any act with intention to fulfill his unnatural lust. 60.After considering the fact and circumstances of the case and relying on the testimony of prosecutrix, this court comes to the conclusion that prosecution has proved its case against accused Dhinesh @ Ravi beyond reasonable doubt for the offence u/s 363/354 IPC . Accordingly, this court held guilty accused Dhinesh @ Ravi for the offence u/s 363 / 354 IPC and acquit him from charges u/s 367 IPC. PRONOUNCED IN THE OPEN COURT ON THIS 20.10.2012.
