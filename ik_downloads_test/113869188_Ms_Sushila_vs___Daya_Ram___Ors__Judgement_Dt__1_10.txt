This is a criminal appeal under Section 372 (proviso) CrPC directed against the judgement dated 31.7.2017 passed by Sh. Vishal Gogne, Ld. Chief CA No. 121/2017 Page 1 of 7 Ms Sushila Vs. Daya Ram & Ors. Judgement dt. 1.10.2018 Metropolitan Magistrate, (North), Rohini, Delhi acquitting the respondent under Section 341 / 324 / 34 IPC, FIR No. 197/2000, PS Alipur. 2. An FIR No. 197/2000 was lodged by appellant in Police Station Alipur alleging that on 26.5.2000 at about 10:00 am, she was present at her matrimonial home and was washing utensils. Her mother­in­law (Saas) Smt. Mewa Devi (respondent no.2 herein) hit her with a bucket (Balti) on her back and also slapped her daughter Frooti. In the meantime, appellant's sister­in­law Anita (respondent n.3) and appellant's father­in­law Daya Ram (respondent no.1) also started beating her. Anita caused injuries on the person of appellant with a sharp edged object. 3. Appellant was got medically examined. Case was investigated and charge sheet was filed in the court of Ld. Metropolitan Magistrate. 4. The perusal of trial court record shows that accused persons had filed a certified copy of judgement dated 16.12.2014 before the trial court and claimed benefit of double jeopardy. The perusal of this judgement shows that Smt. Sushila (Appellant herein) had married Manoj Kumar on 26.1.1992 but the matrimonial relations became strained and she lodged an FIR No. 499/2000 in police station Ashok Vihar under Section 498A / 406 / 34 IPC against her husband Manoj Kumar, her sister­in­law Sunita, her brother­in­law Naresh as well as respondent no.1, 2 & 3 herein. It was alleged in the said FIR that the accused persons were making unlawful demands of dowry and are treating her with cruelty. 5. The charge sheet in case FIR No. 499/2000 was filed in Mahila Court, North West, Rohini, Delhi. A charge under Section 498A / 406 / 34 IPC was framed CA No. 121/2017 Page 2 of 7 Ms Sushila Vs. Daya Ram & Ors. Judgement dt. 1.10.2018 against six accused persons including the respondent no.1, 2 & 3 herein. Vide judgement dated 16.12.2014, Ms Shefali Barnala Tandon convicted all the accused persons under Section 498A / 34 IPC. However, she acquitted all of them under Section 406 / 34 IPC. The perusal of the judgement shows that in her testimony as PW3, Smt. Sushila narrated various instances of cruelty including the incident dated 26.5.2000 in respect of which FIR No. 197/2000 was lodged in police station Alipur. This judgement also shows that PW14 SI Jogender Singh proved the FIR no. 197/2000, PS Alipur and in last portion of para 8, there is reference of this FIR. 6. After hearing both the parties in the present case i.e. FIR No. 197/2000, Ld. Metropolitan Magistrate acquitted respondent no.1, 2 & 3 holding that the trial of the accused persons is in violation of Section 300 CrPC as it would amount to re­appreciate the facts, which have formed the basis of judgement in FIR No. 499/2000. Accordingly, Ld. Chief Metropolitan Magistrate acquitted all the accused persons by affording them statutory protection Section 300 CrPC. 7. Aggrieved by this order, complainant Smt. Sushila has filed the present appeal under proviso 2 Section 372 CrPC, empowered the victim to file an appeal. 8. Ld. Counsel for respondents has argued that Ld. Metropolitan Magistrate has rightly relied upon Rafiq Khan & Anr. Vs. Smt. Jamila Bee & Anr. 1(1999) DMC 448 of High Court of Madhya Pradesh. Ld. Counsel for respondents has also referred to Sarbjit Singh Vs. State of Punjab 715 Recent Criminal Reports 1990(1), Kuljit Singh Vs. Jasbir Singh 2002(3) CCJ 349 and CA No. 121/2017 Page 3 of 7 Ms Sushila Vs. Daya Ram & Ors. Judgement dt. 1.10.2018 Jitender Kumar Nagar Vs. State of Haryana 92 Recent Criminal Reports 1993(2) of Punjab and Haryana High Court in his support. He also cited Mahinder Kumar Vs. State 2003(1) CCJ 190 of Delhi High Court. Ld. Counsel for respondents further cited Challa Sriniwas Vs. Smt. Challa Aruna 2004(2) CCJ 339 of Orissa High Court. Accordingly, Ld. Counsel for respondents strongly defended the impugned judgement. 9. On the other hand Ld. Counsel for appellant has cited Sangeetaben Mahendra Bhai Patel Vs. State of Gujarat & Anr. (2012) 7 Supreme Court Cases 621 and The State of Bombay Vs. S. L. Apte & Anr. AIR 1962 Supreme Court 478 and has argued that Hon'ble Supreme Court has clearly held that while deciding the question of double jeopardy, the emphasis is not on facts rather on the ingredients, which constitute two offences. Ld. Counsel for appellant submits that the judgements of various High Courts are contrary to the aforesaid judgemens of Supreme Court. 10. Ld. Additional Public Prosecutor has also taken the stand in favour of the appellant. 11. Ld. Counsel for respondents has taken me to the judgement passed by Ms Shefali Barnala Tandon, Ld. MM in case FIR No. 499/2000, PS Ashok Vihar, under Section 498A / 406 / 34 IPC. In this judgement, Ld. MM discussed the evidence of PW3 Sushila (appellant herein) in detail. While she testified the detail of various incidents, which happened to her, she also made specific reference to the incident dated 26.5.2000 (which is also subject matter of case FIR No. 197/2000, PS Alipur, under Section 324 IPC). Ld. Counsel for respondents has CA No. 121/2017 Page 4 of 7 Ms Sushila Vs. Daya Ram & Ors. Judgement dt. 1.10.2018 taken me to the judgement of Ms Shefali Barnala Tandon, Ld. MM, (certified copy of which was placed in the trial court record) and has pointed out that the concerned witness i.e. ASI Karampal was examined in the said trial as PW13 and he also proved the FIR no. 197/2000, PS Alipur as Ex.PW3/A. Further, SI Jogender Singh was examined as PW14, who testified that he had met Ms Sushila and recorded her statement, got her medically examined and got FIR No. 197/2000, PS Alipur registered under Section 324 / 34 IPC. It is further argued that Ms Shefali Barnala Tandon, Ld. MM has specifically observed in judgement that the complaint of Sushila, an FIR No. 197/2000 have been well proved and PW14 SI Jogender Singh had got the complainant medically examined. Therefore, Ld. Metropolitan Magistrate had given findings on the incident in respect of which FIR No. 197/2000 was registered. It is therefore submitted that the incident dated 26.5.2000 has also been decided by ms Shefali Barnala Tandon, Ld. Metropolitan Magistrate and consequently Sh. Vishal Gogne, Ld. Metropolitan Magistrate has rightly given the protection under Section 300 CrPC to the respondents in the present case. 12. On the contrary, it is argued by Ld. Counsel for appellant as well as Ld. Additional Public Prosecutor that neither a charge under Section 324 IPC was framed in the trial of case FIR No. 499/2000, PS Ashok Vihar, nor Ld. Metropolitan Magistrate had convicted the appellant under Section 324 IPC. It is argued that ingredients are different from Section 498A IPC. 13. I have carefully perused the judgement passed by Ms Shefali Barnala Tandon, Ld. Metropolitan Magistrate. Although, she has referred to the incident CA No. 121/2017 Page 5 of 7 Ms Sushila Vs. Daya Ram & Ors. Judgement dt. 1.10.2018 dated 26.5.2000 but careful perusal of the judgement shows that Ms Shefali Barnala Tandon, Ld. Metropolitan Magistrate has only made a passing reference to the same and has observed in last portion of para 8 of the judgement that "complaint and FIR no. 197/2000 have been well proved". She has not observed as to whether the allegations made in the complaint and FIR have been proved or not. Ld. Metropolitan Magistrate had been circumspect in giving a specific finding in respect of the incident dated 26.5.2000. Reason is clear. The said incident was subject matter of the trial of case FIR No. 197/2000, PS Alipur. I also agree with Ld. Counsel for appellant and Ld. Additional Public Prosecutor that ingredients of Section 498A IPC and Section 324 IPC are different. So far as the case law relied upon by Ld. Metropolitan Magistrate in the impugned judgment and by Ld. Additional Public Prosecutor are concerned, I am of the opinion that the said judgments would have been applicable if Ms Shefali Barnala Tandon, Ld. MM had given a specific finding that the accused persons had committed offence under Section 324 IPC. As I have already stated that there is only a passing reference made by Ld. Metropolitan Magistrate regarding this incident, although Smt. Sushila had narrated this incident in her testimony as PW3. 14. Consequently, I hold that the trial of case FIR No. 197/2000, PS Alipur is not hit by Section 300 Cr.PC. The impugned judgment is set aside and appeal is allowed. The trial court is directed to hear final arguments and deliver its findings purely on the basis of the evidence obtaining on judicial file. CA No. 121/2017 Page 6 of 7 Ms Sushila Vs. Daya Ram & Ors.
