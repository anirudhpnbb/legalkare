Being a criminal trial, it is for the complainant to prove her case beyond all reasonable doubts by leading reliable, cogent and convincing evidence and it is for the complainant to ensure that her case is able to stand on its own legs. The complainant cannot derive any benefit whatsoever from the weakness of the defence of the accused if any. Accused is entitled to the benefit of every reasonable doubt in the version of the complainant. 22. In respect of the commission of the offence under Section 506 IPC, during her post charge evidence, the complainant/CW1 Anju has testified that on 07.07.1995 at about 11.30 am at her paternal house, the accused accompanied with one more person had threatened her to withdraw the case or else she will face dire consequences. However, there is no further evidence on record whether as a CIS No. 3101/2016 Anju Sharma Vs. Shashi Pal Sharma PS Ashok Vihar Page 9 of 15 consequence of such threat, any alarm had been caused to the complainant within the meaning of Section 503 IPC. It is duly noted that while in the initial complaint, the complainant had stated that she had refused for MLC in respect of the incident dated 12.07.1995, due to shock consequent from the threat of the accused, her entire post-charge evidence is conspicuously silent as to the same which constitutes a material omission and in the absence of any explanation regarding the same, merely on the basis of the vague threat of dire consequences, the ingredients of section 506 IPC remain unfulfilled the benefit of which accrues to the accused. 23. In respect of the allegations of defamation, the complainant/CW1 Anju has testified in her post charge evidence that on 07.07.1995 at about 11.30 am at her paternal house, the accused had stated that he had already informed her neighbors that she was a mad lady. Even CW2 Pooja Mishra @ Pooja Sharma has also testified that on 07.07.1995 at about 11.30-11.45 am, the accused had asked her to depose against the complainant as she was unsound mind lady. 24. It is duly noted that to constitute an offence under Section 499 IPC punishable under section 500 IPC, it was for the complainant to prove not only that the accused had published by spoken words an imputation concerning her but also to prove that such imputation was with the intent to harm or with the knowledge or with reason to believe that it will harm her reputation or defame her. The harm envisioned in this section thus refers to an imputation on a person's CIS No. 3101/2016 Anju Sharma Vs. Shashi Pal Sharma PS Ashok Vihar Page 10 of 15 character made and expressed to others which lowers him not in his own estimation but lowers him in their reputation. 25. In the present case however, the only words attributed to the accused are that he had told CW2 Pooja that the complainant was of unsound mind. There is no further evidence led by the complainant as to the said utterances having defamed her in any manner or as to such words/utterances having in any manner lowered the reputation of complainant in the estimation of CW2 Pooja or anyone else. Rather, it has come on record in the testimony of CW2 Pooja that she had categorically told the accused that she could not help him to depose falsely against Anju Sharma which implies that the reputation of the complainant was not lowered in the estimation of CW2 Pooja and remained intact. No other neighbour has been examined by the complainant at the post charge evidence to corroborate the case of the complainant as her reputation having been lowered in their estimation or as to the complainant having been defamed by the utterances of the accused the benefit of which omission accrues to the accused. 26. It is further noted that as CW1/the complainant Anju has also testified that on 12.07.1995, at about 1.30pm, when she was waiting for the school bus of her nephew, the same person who had accompanied the accused on 07.07.1995 came on a scooter and hit her due to which she fell and sustained injuries. Even CW2/ Pooja has testified on similar lines as to the complainant having CIS No. 3101/2016 Anju Sharma Vs. Shashi Pal Sharma PS Ashok Vihar Page 11 of 15 intentionally been hit by a scooter being driven by the same person who had accompanied the accused on 07.07.1995 due to which the complainant fell and was not able to stand. 27. However, the testimony of the complainant/CW1 and that of CW2/Pooja is not consistent in material aspect casting doubt as to the case of the complainant. For instance, the manner in which the complainant had been hit has been rendered doubtful since during her cross-examination, CW1 has testified that she had been hit from the front which is contrary to the testimony of CW2 Pooja who has categorically testified in her cross-examination that the scooter had hit her from behind. The complainant herself has admitted in her cross-examination that she had not filed any site plan of the place where the incident took place and in the absence of the same, the manner of incident remains unproved even more so when neither of the complainant witnesses have testified as to the direction from which the said scooter was purportedly coming nor it has come on record as to in which direction he fled subsequently which is important to assess whether the complainant witnesses were even in a position to have seen the scooterist before or even after the purported incident/accident. 28. It is also duly noted that the testimony of the complainant witnesses is also inconsistent in respect of other aspects which further renders the case of the complainant doubtful. For instance, the complainant/ CW1 Anju has testified that the driver of the scooter had not worn CIS No. 3101/2016 Anju Sharma Vs. Shashi Pal Sharma PS Ashok Vihar Page 12 of 15 the helmet but he was the same person who had come with the accused on 07.07.1995. However, the complainant has herself testified that she had been informed by her neighbors that the scooterist was the same person who had given threats on 07.07.1995 to kill her. Now it does not appeal to reason that if the scooterist was not wearing a helmet and had hit the complainant from the front, then what was the occasion for the neighbors to tell the complainant as to whether the scooterist was the same person who had accompanied the accused on 07.07.1995 when the complainant has claimed in her examination in chief that the accused accompanied with an accomplice had rang her bell on 07.07.1995 thereby giving her sufficient opportunity to see the said accomplice. 29. The identification of the scooterist as an accomplice of the accused is further rendered doubtful since CW2 Pooja has taken a contrary stand as she has testified that the scooterist had been wearing a helmet and the accused was also riding as a pillion rider on the scooter which was never the case of the complainant and thus it renders the testimony of CW2 doubtful even more so, as CW2 Pooja has also not supported the case of the complainant since she has categorically testified that the complainant was returning after seeing the demolition drive when she was hit from behind which is inconsistent with the testimony of the complainant as to her standing at the bus stop of her nephew when she was hit from front. It is also duly noted that as CW1, the complainant Anju had initially testified that the accused had given the indication to the person accompanying him on 07.07.1995 to do something against her. In her further examination in chief she improved upon the same and testified that the accused had instructed the person who had accompanied him 'mauka dekhke iska kaam tamam kar dena'. However, her improved testimony is inconsistent with her own complaint Ex CW1/1 as per which she had alleged that the accused had instructed the accompanying person 'muka dekh kar yeh kaam kar dena'. Hence, there is no mention in Ex CW1/1 as to the accused having instructed the said person to kill the complainant or to do anything against the complainant and hence her argument as to the incident dated 12.07.1995 being a result of abetment of accused does not inspire confidence of the Court with the complicity of the accused having remained unproved therein. 31. It is also pertinent to note here that while the complainant has relied upon the OPD card ie Mark A to prove the factum of injuries having been caused to her in the incident dated 12.07.1995, she took no further steps to prove the same during the post charge evidence rendering Mark A as unproved and the same cannot be looked into. It is also duly noted that despite testifying in her cross-examination that she had sustained fracture on right leg, the complainant did not prove on record to corroborate the nature of injury and hence sufficient evidence has not come on record to prove that any injury resulted to the complainant as a result of any act of the accused and CIS No. 3101/2016 Anju Sharma Vs. Shashi Pal Sharma PS Ashok Vihar Page 14 of 15 consequently the benefit of the doubt accrues to the accused. Decision 32. In view of aforesaid facts and circumstances, from the evidence as led, as the complainant has failed to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt and hence accused Shashi Pal Sharma, S/o Sh. Bal Mukund Sharma is hereby acquitted for the offence under Section 323 / 109 / 506 / 500 IPC in the present case. 33.
