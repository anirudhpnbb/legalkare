Respondents Date of filing of revision : 27.09.2017 Date of allocation : 03.10.2017 Arguments concluded on : 14.12.2017 Date of order : 03.01.2018 CR No. 51/17 (351/2017) 03.01.2018 Page no. 1/8 Revision petition under section 397 and 399 Cr.P.C against the order dated 27.07.2017 passed by ld. MM­05, (South) New Delhi dismissing the criminal complaint filed by the revisionist against the respondents. ORDER 1. This criminal revision u/s 397 and 399 of the Code of Criminal Procedure is directed against the order dated 27.07.2017 passed by the Ld. MM­05, South District, New Delhi whereby the complaint filed by the revisionist against the respondents Shashi Vatan, Vikrant Aneja and T. C. Aneja for criminal defamation was dismissed. 2. The facts relevant for the disposal of the petition are that a case vide FIR No. 101/2006 was registered against some officers in the Anti Corruption branch of Delhi Police, Old Secretariat in which the revisionist was one of the accused. He was arrested on 29.01.2009 i.e about two days before his retirement. His house was searched on 30.01.2009. It was alleged that when the revisionist was in judicial custody from 05.02.2009 to 16.03.2009, the respondent Shashi Vatan on willful/active pursuance, inducement and instigation of Vikrant Aneja and T C Aneja lodged a false complaint to the DCP vide Diary No. 416 dated 06.03.2009 that the complainant had threatened her, her nephew and her father with licensed arm with dire consequences. It was alleged that after his release from the jail, CR No. 51/17 (351/2017) 03.01.2018 Page no. 2/8 he was called in the police station Neb Sarai and his license of arm/revolver was suspended thus exposing him to future prospect of endangerment of his safety and security. He filed an RTI application on 23.04.2009 and the DCP in his reply dated 14.05.2009 informed him that Smt. Shashi Vatan did not give any call details nor any particular number, date & time and place or the witnesses etc as to the threat to her and her father. He filed another RTI application on 05.06.2009 and vide reply dated 03.07.2009, he was informed that as per the report of the ACP Mehrauli, enquiry into the matter was conducted and no cognizance offence was found to have been made out and as such the same was filed. However no notice u/s 182 IPC was given to Shashi Vatan, Vikrant Aneja and T C Aneja. It was alleged that the revisionist was prosecuted in various false cases by the respondent Shashi Bala Aneja. He then filed the complaint praying that criminal defamation be initiated against the respondents. 3. The revisionist examined himself as CW1. The Ld. MM vide impugned order observed that the revisionist/complainant has neither proved the alleged false complaint dated 06.03.2009 nor the alleged replies received by him with respect to the applications filed under RTI. Reference was also made to the explanation of Section 499 and it was observed that the complainant/revisionist neither mentioned the names of the persons nor examined anyone who could depose that in his CR No. 51/17 (351/2017) 03.01.2018 Page no. 3/8 estimation, the reputation of the revisionist/complainant was lowered due to such complaint. It was observed that merely by making a bald assertion by himself that his reputation was tarnished in the estimation of some people, it cannot be said that his reputation was harmed. It was held that no case u/s 499 IPC is made out. 4. This order is assailed on the ground that the complaint to the DCP was made by the respondent No. 1 on 06.03.2009 that her old father aged 65 years and her nephew ( adult) are in danger as the revisionist has been threatening them frequently and his licensed revolver emboldened him to be frustrated and desperate, although on 06.03.2009, the revisionist was in jail. Further, the enquiry conducted by DCP shows that no criminal offence was made out which goes to show that the complaint was pre­planned and false with a motive to defame and disrepute the revisionist in the eyes of his neighbours, society, relatives and friends by projecting him as a assaulter and in the habit of threatening with the revolver; that on account of the false complaint, the license of the weapon was suspended by the DCP resulting into lowering the reputation and exposing the revisionist who is a senior citizen aged 69 years and a practicing advocate and further, his physical security and safety is at stake. 5. The respondent No. 2/Vikrant Aneja contested the petition and filed the written submissions stating that the complainant failed to bring any material to show that the police complaint dated CR No. 51/17 (351/2017) 03.01.2018 Page no. 4/8 06.03.2009 filed by Shashi Aneja was false. It is stated that there is no incriminating evidence against the respondents nor there is any illegality or infirmity in the order of Ld MM. It is stated that merely filing of documents alongwith the complaint cannot be a substitute for proving the same in accordance with the Indian Evidence Act . Reference was made of the case Pepsi Foods Limited & another v. Special Judicial Magistrate & Others (1998) 5 SCC 749 wherein it was observed that summoning of an accused in a criminal case is a serious matter and a criminal law cannot be set into motion as a matter of course. 6. I have heard the arguments advanced by the revisionist himself and Sh. Saurabh Munjal, Ld. counsel for the respondents. 7. Perusal of the record reveals that the respondent No. 1 was the wife of the complainant/revisionist. Various litigations are pending between the revisionist/complainant and respondent No. 1. Respondent No. 2 is her nephew. Respondent No. 3 is her father who has since been expired. A case vide FIR 01/2006 was registered against the revisionist/complainant by the anti corruption branch u/s 409 / 120B IPC in which he was arrested. He remained in jail from 30.01.2009 to 16.03.2009. The record reveals that the revisionist/complainant had a license of arm. In the complaint, the respondent No. 1 has given specific instances as to the threats given by the revisionist. She never alleged that CR No. 51/17 (351/2017) 03.01.2018 Page no. 5/8 the alleged threats were of the period when the revisionist was in jail. Her complaint reveals that she had also lodged a complaint of threats on 18.04.2007. 8. It is true that on that complaint, the revisionist was called in the police station but record shows that there are other cases registered against him including the case u/s 409 / 420 / 120B IPC vide FIR No. 01/2006, FIR No. 84/08, FIR No. 576/07 u/s 406/498A IPC and under Domestic Violence Act besides other cases. It is not the case that on the said complaint only, the license of the revisionist was suspended. 9. It was rightly observed by the Ld MM that the complainant did not prove the alleged false complaint and the replies received by him with respect to his RTI applications though he was legally bound. He did not examine any person/relative as to how his reputation was lowered in the society due to such complaint. Since no cognizable offence was found to have been made on the complaint allegedly given by respondent No. 1, no action was taken but there was no observation during enquiry that no such incident had happened. The allegations appear to be bald and does not constitute the offence as defined u/s 499 IPC punishable u/s 500 IPC. It appears that the said complaint was the counter blast of the complaints/cases registered against the revisionist at the instance of the respondent No. 1. The case of Laxmi Narayan Gupta v. Commissioner of Police 2006 Law CR No. 51/17 (351/2017) 03.01.2018 Page no. 6/8 Suit (Del) 929 and Soman v. State of Kerala (2013) 11 SCC 382 do not help the revisionist in any manner as the offence punishable u/s 500 IPC is non­cognizable. The case of Laxmi Narayan supra talks about the legal position that the police is under a statutory obligation to register the First Information Report and take up the investigation if the acts complained prima facie disclose several cognizable offences. The case of Soman supra talks about determining the appropriate sentence for the offence committed. The instant case related to the complaint filed by the revisionist for defamation which is non­ cognizable offence and it was at the stage of summoning only. 10. In the case of Pepsi Foods Limited & another v. Special Judicial Magistrate & Others (1998) 5 SCC 749, it has been held "... Summoning of an accused in a criminal case is a serious matter. Criminal law cannot be set into motion as a matter of course. It is not that the complainant has to bring only two witnesses to support his allegations in the complaint to have the criminal law set into motion. The order of the Magistrate summoning the accused must reflect that he has applied his mind to the facts of the case and the law applicable thereto. He has to examine the nature of allegations made in the complaint and the evidence both oral and documentary in support thereof and would that be sufficient for the complainant to succeed in bringing charge home to the accused. It is not that the Magistrate is a silent spectator at the time of recording of preliminary evidence before summoning of the accused. Magistrate has to CR No. 51/17 (351/2017) 03.01.2018 Page no. 7/8 carefully scrutinise the evidence brought on record and may even himself put questions to the complainant and his witnesses to elicit answers to find out the truthfulness of the allegations or otherwise and then examine if any offence is prima facie committed by all or any of the accused.." 11. For the aforesaid reasons, I do not find any infirmity or illegality in the order of the Trial Court which calls for interference by this Court. 12. The revision petition is accordingly dismissed with no orders as to the cost. 13. Trial Court Record be sent back with the copy of this order. 14. File of revision petition be consigned to the Record Room.
