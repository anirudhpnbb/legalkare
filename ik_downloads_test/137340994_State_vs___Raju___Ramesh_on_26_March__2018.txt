I have heard the rival submissions and carefully perused the material available on record. FIR No. 434/04 State Vs Raju @ Ramesh Page 10 of 16 PS : NDRS 21. In a criminal case the burden is on the prosecution to prove its case beyond reasonable doubts before the accused is asked to put his defence. 22. In the present case, the accused has been charged for committing the offences punishable under Section 420 / 468 / 471 / 34 IPC & Section 144 the Railway Act, 1989. 23. Section 144 , the Railways Act provides prohibition of hawking etc. and begging. Section 420 IPC provides punishment for cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property. In order to constitute, offence under Section 420 IPC, the prosecution has to establish that the accused had deceived the complainant/victim dishonestly inducing him to part with any property in his favour which he would not have parted but for the deception played on him. Thus, the essential ingredients of the offence are that there must be dishonest intention on the part of the accused at the time of making the representation to the complainant on the basis of which the complainant part with his property. Intention must be dishonest and there must also be mens rea. 24. Section 468 , IPC provides punishment for committing forgery with intention that the document or FIR No. 434/04 State Vs Raju @ Ramesh Page 11 of 16 PS : NDRS electronic record forged shall be used for the purpose of cheating. 25. Section 471 , IPC provides punishment for using as genuine a forged document or electronic record. 26. Section 34 , IPC provides that when a criminal act is done by several persons in furtherance of their common intention, each of such person is liable for that act in the same manner as if it were done by him alone. It is not necessary that there must be prior conspiracy or pre meditation as the common intention can be formed in the course of occurrence. 27. In the present case, it is alleged by the prosecution that accused Raju @ Ramesh, in furtherance of common intention with accused Gurmeet Singh (since PO) had deceived the complainant and induced him to pay an amount which was more than the fare of the taxi. It is further alleged that the accused had prepared a forged bill for the purpose of cheating the complainant and that the said bill was used as genuine even though there was knowledge that it was forged document. However, perusal of the record and the testimonies of the witnesses would show that the prosecution has failed to establish beyond reasonable doubts the role of accused Raju in any of such FIR No. 434/04 State Vs Raju @ Ramesh Page 12 of 16 PS : NDRS transactions. There is nothing on Court Record to prove beyond reasonable doubts that accused Raju @ Ramesh was part of preparation of alleged forged bill of Rs.325/­. Further, there is nothing on Court record to prove beyond reasonable doubt that accused Raju @ Ramesh had prepared the forged bill as alleged or that he had used any forged document as genuine. 28. The only eye witness of the incident is PW­1 R.K. Mukharjee, who is also the complainant. In his examination­in­chief, he has stated that there was a person in plain clothes in the prepaid booth who had directed him towards a taxi bearing registration no. HR­38C­T0363 and said that payment of Rs.325/­ had to be made. He was told that that bill would be given on reaching at the destination. After reaching at the destination, he had demanded the receipt and the driver had given the bill Ex.PW2/A. The witness, in his examination, has categorically stated that he did not know whether accused Raju was present at the alleged place on the relevant date and time. He has further stated that accused Raju was not present at the alleged time and that he had seen him for the first time in the Court. Thus, as per the testimony of PW­1, the eye witenss, accused Raju was not present at the spot. There is nothing on record to show that accused Raju FIR No. 434/04 State Vs Raju @ Ramesh Page 13 of 16 PS : NDRS @ Ramesh was indulge in any act of canvassing, hawking or for any other purpose at the railway station on the relevant date and time. Therefore, accused Raju @ Ramesh cannot be convicted for an offence under Section 144 , the Railways Act. 29. Further, there is nothing on Court record to show that accused Raju @ Ramesh had acted with accused Gurmeet Singh to cheat or deceive the complainant. As per the complainant himself, he had seen accused Raju @ Ramesh in the Court only. The allegation of the prosecution is that accused Raju @ Ramesh is the registered owner of the vehicle bearing no. HR­38C­T0363 which was used by the driver to carry the complainant from NDRS to his home. However, there is nothing on Court record to prove beyond reasonable doubts that the said vehicle was the same vehicle which was used by the alleged person to carry the complainant from New Delhi Railway Station to his Home at Sector 44, Noida. The vehicle was not seized from the spot. I find merits in the submissions of learned defence counsel that it might be possible that the forged number plate was used on the vehicle which was used to carry the complainant. There is nothing on Court record to show that accused Gurmeet Singh (since PO) and accused Raju @ Ramesh are known FIR No. 434/04 State Vs Raju @ Ramesh Page 14 of 16 PS : NDRS to each other and that accused Raju @ Ramesh had kept Gurmeet Singh (since PO) as a driver on his vehicle. Nothing has been recovered from the possession or at the instance of accused Raju. The alleged disclosure statement which is Ex.PW­5/E is inadmissible in evidence under Section 25 of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872 and no part of the statement is admissible under Section 27 of the Act. 30. Other witnesses are only formal witnesses. None of these witnesses have made any allegations against accused Raju. PW­9 Mahinder Pal Singh who is public witness has stated that he did not know accused Raju @ Ramesh. Therefore, statement of this witness also does not prove anything against the accused. Entire case of the prosecution against accused Raju @ Ramesh is based on speculation that the car belonging to accused Raju had been used in committing the offences. However, even this fact that the car of accused Raju @ Ramesh was used has not been proved beyond reasonable doubts. The other ingredients of the offences also have not been proved beyond reasonable doubts against the accused. The accused is entitled to the reasonable doubts. Hence, the accused is given the benefits of doubts and he is accordingly acquitted of the alleged offences. FIR No. 434/04 State Vs Raju @ Ramesh Page 15 of 16 PS : NDRS 31.
