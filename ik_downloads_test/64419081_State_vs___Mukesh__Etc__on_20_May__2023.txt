It is fundamental principle of criminal jurisprudence that an accused is presumed to be innocent and therefore, the burden lies on the prosecution to prove the guilt of the accused persons beyond reasonable doubt. The general burden of establishing the guilt of accused persons are always on the prosecution and it never shifts. 16. I have thoughtfully considered the arguments advanced, perused the material available on record, scrutinized the evidence led by the prosecution and gone through the relevant provisions of law. For the sake of convenience, I shall be discussing the culpability of accused u/s 341 & 323 of IPC separately. CULPABILITY OF ACCUSED U/S 341 IPC 17. Section 341 IPC provides the punishment for offense under section 339 IPC. Section 339 of IPC reads as under:- 339. Wrongful restraint.--Whoever voluntarily obstructs any person so as to prevent FIR No. 119/2011 PS SP Badli State V. Mukesh Etc Page No. 7 of 13 that person from proceeding in any direction in which that person has a right to proceed, is said wrongfully to restrain that person. The ingredients of offense under Section 339 IPC are as under:- a) That there was an obstruction; b) That the obstruction prevented the complainant from proceeding in any direction; c) That the person/complainant so proceeding must have a right to proceed in the direc- tion concerned. 18. It was for the prosecution to establish that the accused persons had caused any obstruction to the victim. To establish that the prosecution relied upon the testimony of PW1 i.e. Hawa Singh complainant who is the star witness. As per the deposition of the witness he was going to his old house for lighting Dipak and accused persons illegally obstructed his way. The witness has nowhere clarifies as to how the accused persons have obstructed his way. Complainant has simply made a bald statement that he was illegally obstructed by the accused persons. Further the complaint Ex. PW1/A which was given by the complainant to the police is also not clear regarding the fact how the accused persons have obstructed his way. Without any clarification it is very difficult to made out the offense of wrongful restraint. There is no other evidence on record to lead the Court to any inference about any obstruction caused by the accused persons which had prevented the complainant from proceeding in any direction. Therefore, due to lack of any evidence, the prosecution has failed to establish the commission of offense of FIR No. 119/2011 PS SP Badli State V. Mukesh Etc Page No. 8 of 13 wrongful restraint. CULPABILITY OF ACCUSED U/S 323 IPC 19. It is now remaining to see that whether the prosecution has established the com- mission of offense under Section 323 IPC. Section 323 of the IPC reads as under:- 323. Punishment for voluntarily causing hurt.--Whoever, except in the case provided for by section 334 , voluntarily causes hurt, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to one year, or with fine which may extend to one thousand rupees, or with both. 20. In light of the above-mentioned provision, in order to prove the commission of the offense under Section 323 of the Indian Penal Code, 1860 by the accused persons, it must be proved that (a) the accused caused either bodily pain or disease or infirmity to the injured; (b) such bodily pain or disease or infirmity was caused by the accused (i) intentionally, or (ii) with the knowledge that his act shall cause such hurt. 21. The prosecution has examined one witness− PW1 who cogently stated that the ac- cused persons starting beating him by fist and blows and danda and that he sustained in- juries on his finger, shoulder and back part of the body because of that.. The MLC of the FIR No. 119/2011 PS SP Badli State V. Mukesh Etc Page No. 9 of 13 complainant was duly exhibited as Ex. PW2/A. PW-2 Dr. Deepti Bhalla, CMO, BSA Hospital deposed that the injuries recorded in the MLC are as follows : (i) Superficial abrasion approx. 1x1 cm in size was present over the medial aspect of right arms. (ii) Tenderness was present over the right shoulder. Bruises were present over the right shoulder and upper aspect of right arms. PW-2 proved MLC Ex. PW2/A bearing the signatures of Dr. Rahul Arora at point A and her signatures at point B. It is pertinent to mention that PW-2 was not cross examined by the Ld. Counsel for accused persons despite the opportunity being given. The witness was not cross-examined by ld. Counsel for the accused. Since, PW-2 was not cross examined by the Ld. Counsel for the accused persons then it will presumed that accused persons have accepted the testimony of PW-2 and also the MLC which was duly exhibited. 22. PW-1 has remained consistent with respect to the injuries suffered by him in his testimony and prosecution has proved the MLC. In fact, MLC proves the injuries suffered by the PW-1. Nothing has came on record from the defense side which have rebut the allegations of the complainant regarding non causing of the hurt. Even in the cross examination, the testimony to that regard could not be impeached and the witness withstood the test of cross examination so far as the testimony that the accused persons have caused simple injuries to them. The contention of the defense counsel that no public witness has been examined does not make the case of the prosecution doubtful since the testimony of PW1 is clear and specific with regard to the hurt inflicted upon FIR No. 119/2011 PS SP Badli State V. Mukesh Etc Page No. 10 of 13 him by the accused persons. Further, the accused persons have been unable to bring to fore any material or circumstances upon which the Court may disbelieve the testimony of the injured as the prosecution witnesses. It cannot be gainsaid that the evidence of an injured witness is generally considered to be very reliable and is accorded a special status in law as it is unlikely that he would have spared the actual assailant in order to falsely implicate someone else as he would not like or want to let his actual assailant go unpunished merely to implicate a third person falsely for the commission of the offense. Thus, the evidence of the injured witness should be relied upon unless there are grounds for the rejection of his evidence on the basis of major contradictions and discrepancies therein. 23. It is a settled law that testimony of injured witness is considered to be very reliable and is accorded a special status in law. In the case of Bhajan Singh Vs. State of Haryana (2011) 7 SCC 421, where it was held as follows:- " The evidence of the stamped witness must be given due weightage as his presence on the place of occurrence cannot be doubted. His statement is generally considered to be very reliable and it is unlikely that he has spared the actual assailants in order to falsely implicate someone else. The testimony of an injured witness has its own relevancy and efficacy as he has sustained injuries at the time and place of occurrence and this lands support to his testimony that he was present at the time of occurrence. Thus, the testimony of an injured witness is accorded a special status in law. Such a witness comes with a built in guarantee of his presence at the scene of crime and is unlikely to spare his actual assailants in order to falsely implicate someone. Convincing evidence is required to discredit an injured witness. (vide Jarnail Singh v. State of Punjab (2009) 9 SCC 719; Balraje @ Trimbak v. FIR No. 119/2011 PS SP Badli State V. Mukesh Etc Page No. 11 of 13 State of Maharashtra (2010) 6 SCC 673; Abdul Sayed v. State of Madhya Pradesh (2010) 10 SCC 259)". 24. In the case of State of U.P. v. Naresh & Ors. (2011) 4 SCC 324, it was held as under:- "The evidence of an injured witness stand on different pedestal as compared to any other witness cited by the prosecution as eye witness, who claims to have seen the incident. Where an injured witness clearly names the person and the assault made on him by those persons which is broadly corroborated with what has been found in the medical report, even though there may not be any mathematical precision with regard to the manner of assault, the evidence of an injured eye witness cannot be lightly thrown aside only on certain minor contradictions and omissions. It cannot be a case of some exaggeration or it could even be some discrepancy in recollecting the whole incident with exactitude and certainty but on certain minor discrepancy disbelieving altogether the testimony of injured eye witness, would be against settled principle of appreciation of evidence. 25. In the present case, the accused persons being unable to elicit any material to adversely affect the credibility or veracity of the testimony of the injured witness and in the absence of any major contradiction or discrepancy having been brought forth in their testimony, the testimony of PW1 inspire the confidence of the Court. 26. In view of the above said discussion, the prosecution has failed to prove the charges u/s 341 IPC against the accused persons and successful in proving the charge u/s 323 IPC against the accused persons.
