BRIEF FACTS OF THIS CASE :- 2. Respondent no.1 herein is the complainant, who had filed a complaint dated 11.08.2016 thereby making allegations against three accused persons i.e. petitioner herein and respondents no.2 and 3 herein. This complaint was filed for offence punishable under Section 500 IPC. After hearing submissions, on 22.02.2017 trial court ordered to summon all three accused persons including petitioner herein to face trial for offence under Section 500 IPC. GROUNDS : - 3. Being aggrieved of the decision taken by ld. trial court so as to summon petitioner in this case, petitioner has preferred this petition on the following relevant grounds :- ● That ld. MM while passing the impugned order dated 22.02.2017 proceeded under influence of the complainant, who redundantly mentioned in very first sentence of his complaint that "complainant is retired Additional & Sessions Judge and now is a practicing advocate in all the court of Delhi." ● That ld. MM felt shy to call the complainant, being claimed to be a retired Additional & Session Judge, in witness box to examine-in- chief. That ld. MM committed a grave irregularity in recording pre- summoning evidence under Section 200 Cr.P.C without examining the complainant and proceeded on the basis of affidavit of the Page 2 of 9 (Pulastya Pramachala) Special Judge (PC Act) CBI, East District Karkardooma Courts, Delhi Criminal Revision No.261/2017 complainant, which is not permissible in law/ Criminal Procedure Code . ● That ld. MM manifestly erred in not considering that complainant tried to influence ld. MM by his position and this was the reason for not strictly complying Criminal Procedure Code . That in the judgment titled as M.S. Rohilla v. High Court of Delhi, 129 (2006) DLT 737, High Court of Delhi held that "Sh. M.S. Rohilla was appointed in Delhi Judicial Service in the afternoon of 02.05.1972. Later on, his work was withdrawn on 24.09.2001 and he was thereafter, compulsorily retired in public interest on 28.09.2001." That it is clear from the order/judgment of Supreme Court and High Court of Delhi in the matter of M.S. Rohilla that his service book, ACR for the year 1993, 1994 and 2000 reflecting adverse remark against him for having the 'Doubtful Integrity' while holding the post as the judicial officer, which require the highest honesty and integrity in all purposes. ● That ld. MM did not apply his judicial mind and did not consider the applicable law in as much as alleged offence under Section 499 / 500 IPC is covered by Fifth Exception to Section 499 IPC. "Merits of case decided in Court or conduct of witnesses and other concerned. - It is not defamation to express in good faith any opinion whatever respecting the merit of any case, civil or criminal, which has been decided by a Court of Justice, or respecting the conduct of any person as a party, witness or agent, in any such case, or respecting the character of such person, as far as his character appears in that conduct and no further." ● That ld. "Ninth Exceptions - Imputation made in good faith by person for protection of his or others interest. - It is not defamation to make an imputation on the character of another provide that the imputation to be made in good faith for the protection of the interest of the person making it, or of any other person, or for the public good." ● That ld. During the course of argument, respondent no.1 had taken exception to some pleadings in the revision petition particularly in para V of grounds of revision submitting that such pleadings were made without any relevance and were scandalous in nature. In view of objection raised by respondent no.1, petitioner was directed to file reply so as to take stand in respect of relevance of such pleadings. Thereafter, a reply was filed by petitioner stating that as per his understanding the facts stated in the grounds taken in the petition are relevant and respondent no.1 had averred claiming that he was a person of high prestige being a retired judge. Therefore, petitioner being in denial mode questioned such plea of respondent no.1 and reproduced the excerpts from the orders passed by Supreme Court and High Court of Delhi. 5. On careful scrutiny of the grounds taken in clause IV and clause V, I find that the petitioner has indirectly imputed a motive for ld. MM to pass order in question, going in favour of respondent no.1. Petitioner has pleaded that ld. MM felt shy to call respondent no.1 into witness box to examine him, being influenced from the background of respondent no.1 as retired Additional & Sessions Judge. She did not comply with Criminal Procedure Code . Petitioner went on to reproduce a part of judgment in the case of M.S. Rohilla v. High Court of Delhi, wherein respondent no.1 was the petitioner and the case probably pertained to his service matter. From this judgment, a portion was quoted to show that respondent no.1 was compulsorily retired in public interest on 28.09.2001 and thereafter, petitioner further pleaded that High Court of Delhi had recorded in the ACR of respondent no.1 for the year 1993, 1994 and 2000 about his doubtful Page 5 of 9 (Pulastya Pramachala) Special Judge (PC Act) CBI, East District Karkardooma Courts, Delhi Criminal Revision No.261/2017 integrity. 6. This revision petition has been filed to assail order on summoning of petitioner, in a case for defamation filed by respondent no.1. Such order has to be assailed by pointing out legal infirmity, rather than narrating personal background of respondent no.1 in his service. Whatever had been recorded in the ACR of respondent no.1 and whatever was the reason for compulsory retirement of respondent no.1 from the service, cannot be a relevant criteria to assail the legality of impugned order. However, petitioner has done so, which cannot be justified. In fact, I also find that petitioner under the garb of grounds to assail the legality of the order, has cast indirectly a motive for ld. MM to pass such order, which is also deplorable. Petitioner is warned that he must desist from making scandalous and irrelevant plea. 7. Coming back to the legality of the impugned order, the relevant contention of ld. counsels had been that ld. MM accepted affidavit of respondent no.1, though as per Section 200 Cr.P.C, the complainant respondent no.1 should have been examined on oath. Therefore, there is illegality which was committed by ld. MM being influenced by introduction of respondent no.1. Another relevant ground pleaded before me was that the alleged defamatory statement/averment made in the civil suit by the petitioner is yet to be proved and by virtue of 5th and 9th exception to Section 499 IPC, no case of defamation is made out against the petitioner. Ld. counsel for petitioner referred and relied upon certain case laws to support their argument that affidavit of respondent no.1 could not have been accepted and to submit that summoning order could not have been passed against the petitioner, which are as under :- Page 6 of 9 (Pulastya Pramachala) Special Judge (PC Act) CBI, East District Karkardooma Courts, Delhi Criminal Revision No.261/2017 ● Sri K. Venkataramaiah & Ors. v. Sri Katterao, ILR 2008 KAR 474. ● M/s. Pepsi Foods Ltd. & Anr. v. Special Judicial Magistrate & Ors. Criminal Appeal decided by Supreme Court of India on 04.11.1997 8. On the other hand, respondent no.1 submitted that his non- examination on oath by ld. MM was mere procedural irregularity, which did not cause any prejudice to the petitioner. Therefore, on this ground, the impugned order cannot be interfered with. He further submitted that petitioner made defamatory plea against him in a civil suit filed against his client, without having any basis. In that civil suit, respondent no.1 was not himself a party, therefore, he did not have any locus to challenge such scandalous and defamatory pleading in that civil suit. He further submitted that any exception to Section 499 IPC cannot be invoked at this stage because such exceptions can be available to accused in his defence and the court can take a final decision only after conducting a trial. Respondent no.1 relied upon certain case laws to support his arguments, which are as under :- ● M.N. Damani v. S.K. Sinha & Ors. 2001 IV AD S.C. 407. ● Basab Ghosh v. M/s. Outlook Publishing (India), Crl.M.C. No. 2962/2009, decided on 25.02.2010. ● Vijay Dhanuka etc. v. Najima Mamtaj etc. III (2014) SLT 643. 9. As far as non examination of respondent no.1 on oath is concerned, I find that the judgment passed by Delhi High Court in the case of Basab Ghosh (supra) takes care of the situation involved herein. In that case also, trial court had passed summoning order merely on the basis of affidavit of complainant and while dealing with the same legal question, Delhi High Court observed that though the examination of complainant and his witness can be dispensed with only if the complaint is made by the public servant acting or Page 7 of 9 (Pulastya Pramachala) Special Judge (PC Act) CBI, East District Karkardooma Courts, Delhi Criminal Revision No.261/2017 purporting to act in discharge of his official duties, but non compliance of such provision in itself would not vitiate the subsequent proceedings and the case need not be quashed on this ground only. The court referred to Section 465 Cr.P.C to say that mere irregularity in the procedure cannot be a ground to interfere with an order passed by court of competent jurisdiction. 10.The ratio of judgment passed in the case of M.N. Damani (supra) read with the ratio of judgment passed in Sevakaran Sobhani v. R.K. Kranjia, (1981) 3 SCC 208, leave no doubt that this is not the stage to look into any of the exception to 499 IPC , so as to decide if the alleged defamatory pleading are covered under any of the exceptions. This question can be decided only after holding a trial on the basis of plea taken by accused, evidence led on the record and over all appreciation of the evidence. Therefore, such argument of petitioner cannot be sustained. 11.It is relevant to reproduce the contents of imputations made and published in the said suit, which are as follows :- "a) Moreover, to mislead the public and others, her counsel Mr. M.S. Rohilla has pasted two press (public) notices on the main gate of the suit property (annexure P- 20). These notices have been authenticated by Mr. M.S. Rohilla, advocate, but it is an fraudulent and illegal act of defendant no.1 as well as Mr. M.S. Rohilla........ b) As written with hand on these notices these were published in newspapers dated 04.12.2014 and 06.12.2014. The C.J. Sh. Aashish Gupta in C. S. 217 /2014 in his order dated 24.11.2014 declared plaintiff as landlord and therefore owner. So as on 04.12.2014/06.12.2014 the decision of lower court was in force and thus, this notice is an act of contempt of court decision dated 24.11.2014. Page 8 of 9 (Pulastya Pramachala) Special Judge (PC Act) CBI, East District Karkardooma Courts, Delhi Criminal Revision No.261/2017 c) The above facts mean that defendant no.1 is not landlord or owner and the notice pasted on the gate of suit property is illegal as well as an act of contempt of court. This act has been done by defendant no.1 with malafide intention in connivance with her counsel........" 12.The alleged defamatory pleading made by petitioner in the civil suit against respondent no.1 do prima facie make out a case for offence punishable under Section 500 IPC because the content of the same are directed against respondent no.1, which has the effect to make accused imputation on the character and working of respondent no.1. Therefore, I do not find any infirmity in the impugned order and hence, revision petition is dismissed. 13.TCR be sent back along with copy of order to the trial court.
