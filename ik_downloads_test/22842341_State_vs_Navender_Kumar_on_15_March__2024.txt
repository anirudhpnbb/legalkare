The arguments were not address on behalf of the accused. APPRECIATION OF EVIDENCE: 13. In order to prove the case beyond reasonable doubt, prosecution was required to prove the ingredients u/s 411 IPC beyond reasonable doubt. At this juncture, it is essential to understand what the ingredients are. Section 379 provides for FIR No. 0135/2019 State Vs. Navender Kumar Page 6 of 10 punishment for offence defined u/s 378 IPC. 378 IPC is titled as: Theft: Whoever, intending to take dishonestly any moveable property out of the possession of any person without that person's consent, moves that property in order to such taking, is said to commit theft. Section 411 IPC is defined as Dishonestly receiving stolen property: Whoever dishonestly receives or retains any stolen property, knowing or having reason to believe the same to be stolen property, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both. 14. The bare reading of above two sections would show that for offence u/s 411 IPC, it has to be proved that the stolen moveable property is received or retained by the accused with the knowledge or the reason to believe that the same is stolen. Now to prove that the accused was the one who dishonestly received/retained the case property with the knowledge/reason to believe that it was stolen, the prosecution has relied on the version of PW1 and PW3. The complainant in this case did not appear despite issuance of multiple notices. The case property is stated to be owned by the complainant and Ex. PW3/E is stated to be the bill of the case property. As per Section 60 and 61 of Evidence Act, the oral evidence if any has to be direct and the contents of documents are to be proved by the person who is in the knowledge of the execution of the document. Ex. P1 is the case property but it has not been proved to be the case property by the owner and even Ex. PW3/E has not been proved by the person who either issued the bill or the person FIR No. 0135/2019 State Vs. Navender Kumar Page 7 of 10 in whose favour the bill was issued. Further, CDR details of the case property were obtained but they have not been relied in evidence by the prosecution. The only proof against the accused is his disclosure statement which was recorded pursuant to the alleged possession of the case property with the accused. 15. PW1 and PW3 have said that the number was found activated on the basis of which the accused was called and he was asked to appear in the police station. As explained above, the complainant did not appear to prove that the alleged phone number found to be working, which is alleged to be found in the possession of the accused was her number or that it was the case property of this case. Further, to make discovery of a fact admissible the requirements of section 27 Evidence act are to be fulfilled. Section 27 does not make the evidence of discovery of a fact admissible; if it is admissible it must be under Section 5 . What it makes admissible is the evidence of a statement of the accused leading to the discovery of the fact. Though Section 27 is worded as if it was only an exception to the preceding sections i.e. Sections 25 and 26 , it really is more than that and contains something not within the scope of the preceding sections. As per section 27 only so much of information given by an accused to a police-officer is admissible which may lead to the discovery of facts, like dead body, the clothes of the deceased, the stolen goods, instruments of crimes, etc. In such cases if a discovery was made in consequence of the information, the portion of the information which was corroborated by the discovery could FIR No. 0135/2019 State Vs. Navender Kumar Page 8 of 10 not be said to be wholly false and could therefore be allowed to be proved without any danger of injustice being done to the accused. However, there can be no discovery of a fact about which the police already has information. Their Lordships of the Supreme Court have also observed in Aher Araja Khima v. State of Saurashtra, (S ) AIR 1956 S. C. 217 (D) that the discovery of incriminating articles alleged to have been recovered by the accused is inadmissible in evidence if the police already knew where they were hidden. 16. Further, the information given by the accused u/s 27 Evidence act should directly and distinctly relate to the facts discovered. Where, therefore, a fact has already been discovered, any information given in that behalf afterwards cannot be said to lead to the discovery of the fact. There cannot be a re-discovery when the police is already in knowledge of the information. In the present case, firstly the case property has not been recovered from a place of hiding on the alleged disclosure statement of accused and secondly, the case property has been recovered after the accused came to the police station at the direction of the IO. It is a settled principle that the recovery should be made in the presence of two respectable persons of the society, as directed u/s section 100 (4) of the Cr.P.C., however, in Ex. PW1/A, the signatures are of PW1 and PW3 and the accused. Moreover, if for the sake of argument, the version of the prosecution is believed yet, the prosecution has not proved that the accused had the knowledge or reason to believe that the case property is stolen. In a criminal trial, the burden of proof is on the FIR No. 0135/2019 State Vs. Navender Kumar Page 9 of 10 prosecution and the mens rea of an offence has to be proved during trial. For unexplained reasons, the IO chose not to file the CDR from the date of offence till recovery and for the reasons best known to the prosecution, the witness who could have proved the partial CDR has not been examined. The discovery made pursuant to the alleged disclosure is not admissible as it is in contravention of the settled procedure and the star witness of this case, the complainant never appeared. 17. Thus, in view of the above discussion and due to absence of any evidence to show that the case property was retained/received by the accused with the knowledge or reason to believe that it is stolen property, this Court has no hesitation in holding that the prosecution has failed to prove the offence u/s 411 IPC against the accused and accordingly accused Navender Kumar S/o Sh. Ram Sanjeevan Kumar stands acquitted from this case.
