By way of this petition under Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, the petitioner has prayed to quash and set aside the criminal complaint being numbered as Criminal Case No.1697/2002 pending before the Court of learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Kutch-Bhuj and also the subsequent proceedings initiated thereto qua him. 2.0 The facts in brief giving rise to the filing of the present petition are as under; 2.1 The petitioner is the Chief Editor of the local daily Sandesh , which is published in different Editions from different places within the State of Gujarat. For looking after the day-to-day affairs of the daily, Resident Editors have been appointed for each of the Divisions. Such Resident Editors look after the day-to-day affairs of the Edition being published from the respective Divisions. 2.2 On 10.01.2000 a news item came to be published in the Rajkot Edition of the news-paper, wherein, certain details pertaining to the passing of a Resolution by the Executive Committee of the Bhuj Nagar Palika on the issue of some land was given. In connection with the said news item, on 13.03.2000 a criminal complaint came to be filed against the petitioner herein and one Kirtibhai Jayantbhai Khatri, the Editor of Kutch Mitra newspaper, by respondent no.2 herein, before the Court of learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Bhuj-Kutch u/s.500 & 427 of the Indian Penal Code. The said criminal complaint was numbered as Criminal Case No.1697/2002 and in pursuance thereof, process came to be issued against the petitioner & the other accused person. 2.3 Being aggrieved by the same, the petitioner herein has preferred this petition under Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. 3.0 Mr. Vipul M. Pancholi, learned Advocate for the petitioner, has submitted that the Sandesh local daily has different Editions and that each Edition is published from different places / divisions situated across the State. For each Division, Resident Editors have been appointed, who look after the day-to-day affairs of their respective Divisions. Therefore, the petitioner, who is stationed at Ahmedabad, has no knowledge about the news item in question. At the most, the person who is handling the day-to-day affairs of the news-paper at the Rajkot Division could be said to be liable for publishing the said news item and not the petitioner. Hence, the complaint in question deserves to be quashed and set aside. 3.1 Learned Advocate has further submitted that the criminal complaint in question is an abuse of the process of Court inasmuch as respondent no.2 has also filed a civil suit being Special Civil Suit No.158/2002 against the Resident Editor and the publisher of the Rajkot Edition of the news-paper for getting compensation of Rs.51 Lacs on the ground of defamation. In the said suit, the petitioner has not been made a party, meaning thereby that at the relevant time, respondent no.2 was well aware of the fact that the petitioner was not at all concerned with the publication of the news items in question. Hence, the impugned complaint has been filed with mala fide intention and is a clear abuse of the process of Court. Therefore, the same deserves to be quashed and set aside. 3.2 In support of his submissions, learned Advocate for the petitioner has relied upon a decision of this Court in the case of R.A. Goenka & Ors. v. State of Gujarat & Ors., 1986 G.L.H. (U.J.) 26. In that case, the petitioner was the Editor stationed at Delhi and there was no allegation that the news, which are published at Ahmedabad, are published after bringing them to his notice or that he knew that such a news item was being published. The Court held that it was not possible to say that the petitioner therein is also guilty of an offence punishable u/s.500 IPC and accordingly, the process issued against the petitioner therein was quashed and set aside. 3.3 Learned Advocate has relied upon a decision of the Kerala High Court in the case of V.S. Achuthanandan v. G. Kamalamma & Anr. , 2008 CRI.L.J. 4221. In that case, the petitioner therein was the Chief Editor of a News-paper but, he was not alleged to be responsible for selection of the disputed news article and its printing and publication. The averments contained in the complaint were not found to be sufficient to constitute the essential ingredients of Section 499 IPC and accordingly, the Court held the taking of cognizance by the Magistrate to be not proper. 3.4 Learned Advocate has next relied upon another decision of the Kerala High Court in the case of Mammen Mathew v. N. Radhakrishnan & Anr. , 2008 CRI.L.J. 845. In that case also, a complaint u/s.499 & 500 IPC was filed against the Editor of a news-paper in which a news item was published. It was found that the responsibility of the Editor was only to select the news item to be published and not to make the same. The Court held that the selection of news item cannot amount to making of news which is already made and kept available for selection and consequently, the complaint against the Editor was quashed and set aside. 3.5 Learned Advocate has lastly relied upon an unreported decision of this Court passed in Criminal Misc. Application No.5605/2001 dated 12.10.2007, wherein, this Court has followed the similar laid down by this Court in R.A. Goenka's case (supra). 3.6 Learned Advocate has, therefore, submitted that since the petitioner herein is not responsible with the publication of the news item in question, he could not be made answerable for the same. Hence, the complaint filed by respondent no.2 deserves to be quashed and set aside qua the petitioner. 4.0 Mr. Nalin Thakkar, learned Advocate for respondent no.2, has submitted that the petitioner has suppressed material facts from this Court inasmuch as before filing the impugned criminal complaint, respondent no.2 had issued a Notice dated 18.01.2000 to the petitioner, through an Advocate, in connection with the news item in question but, no reply was given by the petitioner. 4.1 Learned Advocate has submitted that the petitioner has also consistently not remained present before the trial Court and has avoided the service of bailable warrants issued by the trial Court from time to time. 4.2 Learned Advocate has further submitted that the complaint in question was filed in 2000 and this petition came to be filed only in 2007, i.e. after a period of more than six years. No explanation has come from the petitioner with regard to the said delay caused in filing the petition. Therefore, on the ground of delay also, the petition does not deserve to be entertained. 4.3 Learned Advocate has next submitted that the petitioner was the Editor of the Rajkot Edition of the daily at the relevant time and therefore, the criminal complaint & the civil suit were filed against the petitioner at the Rajkot address. Hence, the petitioner was having the knowledge about the publication of the news items in the Rajkot Edition of the news-paper and material facts have been suppressed from this Hon'ble Court. Therefore, this petition does not deserve to be entertained. 4.4 In support of his submissions, learned Advocate for respondent no.2 has relied upon a decision of the Orissa High Court in the case of Masoodul Huq & Ors. v. Umakanta Misra , 1986 CRI.L.J. 1204. In that case, it was found that the article published in a news-paper was found to be defamatory qua the complainant. Therefore, it was held that the order of the Magistrate taking cognizance cannot be set aside u/s.482 Cr.P.C. and that the question whether the printer and publisher are entitled to benefit of Section 499 IPC can only be gone into during trial. 4.5 Learned Advocate has next relied upon a decision of this Court in the case of Smt. Shirinbai Dungaji Daruwala since Decd. By her Heirs Aspi Dungaji Daruwala v. Competent Authority & Dy. Collector, Ahmedabad & Anr. , 1996 (1) G.L.R. 455, wherein, it has been held that an application is liable to be dismissed for suppression of material facts under Article 226 of the Constitution of India. Similar principle has been laid down in another decision of this Court in the case of Vinubhai Malabhai Visani & Ors. v. Lathi Nagar Panchayat & Ors. , 1997 (3) G.L.R. 2205. 4.6 Learned Advocate has lastly relied upon another decision of this Court in the case of Akshar Share Services Pvt. Ltd. & Anr. v. State of Gujarat & Anr. , 1999 (1) G.L.R. 499, more particularly, on the observations made in Para-16 therein, which reads as under; 16. ..........The petitioners have deliberately stated about the applications filed by M. Touch Finance Limited, wherein, the complaints came to be quashed by this Court. The petitioner was bound to disclose the facts of filing of previous applications for identical relief in this Court. In all the present applications, the petitioners have made the statement on oath that no other petition has been filed in this Court or in any other Court in respect of the subject-matter of this petition. This statement is made in spite of the fact that the petitioners had filed previous applications in respect of the complaints filed by the respondents which came to be rejected by this Court. 4.7 Learned Advocate has, therefore, submitted that in view of the suppression of material facts and the making of false averments by the petitioner, this petition deserves to be dismissed since truth could be ascertained only from the trial. 5. Heard learned counsel for the respective parties and perused the documents on record. In the impugned complaint filed u/s.427 & 500 IPC by respondent no.2, the petitioner herein has been arraigned as the main accused, along with one other person. From the record, it appears that the petitioner herein is the Chief Editor of the local daily and that the said daily publishes different Editions from different parts / divisions situated across the State. Every such Division is being managed and controlled by a Resident Editor, who looks after the day-to-day affairs of the daily in such Divisions. 6. The news item in question was published on 10.01.2000 in the Rajkot Edition of the daily. The petitioner herein was not responsible for the publication of the said news item since he was not the Editor of the Rajkot Division and was also not responsible for handling the day-to-day affairs of the said Edition at the relevant time. No specific averments / allegations have been made in the impugned complaint to the effect that the news item in question was published, after bringing it to the knowledge of the petitioner or that the petitioner was aware about the offending news item being published or that the petitioner had any role to play in the selection of such item for publication. 7. In absence of any material disclosed in the complaint and in view of the admitted fact that the petitioner is the Chief Editor of the daily stationed at Ahmedabad and the news item was published in its Rajkot Edition alone, where the news-paper has a separate Resident Editor, the petitioner cannot be proceeded against for the alleged offence in question. Even if the allegations made in the impugned complaint are taken at its face value, they do not constitute any offence. Hence, I am of the opinion that the complaint qua the petitioner herein deserves to be quashed. 8. Considering the facts of the case, it would be relevant to refer to a decision of the Apex Court in the case of State of Haryana & Ors. V. Bhajan Lal & Ors. , 1992 Supp (1) SCC 335, wherein, the Apex Court has broadly set out the circumstances in which the High Court can exercise the power under Section 482 of Criminal Procedure Court. Paragraphs 102 and 103 thereof read as under: The following categories of cases can be stated by way of illustration wherein the extraordinary power under Article 226 or the inherent powers under Section 482 CrPC can be exercised by the High Court either to prevent abuse of the process of any court or otherwise to secure the ends of justice, though it may not be possible to lay down any precise, clearly defined and sufficiently channelized and inflexible guidelines or rigid formula and to give an exhaustive list of myriad kinds of cases wherein such power should be exercised; Where the allegations made in the first information report or the complaint, even if they are taken at their face value and accepted in their entirety do not prima facie constitute any offence or make out a case against the accused. Where the allegations in the FIR do not disclose a cognizable offence, justifying an investigation by police officers under Section 156(1) of the Code except under an order of a Magistrate within the purview of Section 155(2) of the Code. Where the uncontroverted allegations made in the FIR or complaint and the evidence collected in support of the same do not disclose the commission of any offence and make out a case against the accused. Where, the allegations in the FIR do not constitute a cognizable offence but constitute only a non-cognizable offence, no investigation is permitted by a police officer without an order of a Magistrate as contemplated under Section 155(2) of the Code. Where the allegations made in the FIR or complaint are so absurd and inherently improbable on the basis of which no prudent persons can ever reach a just conclusion that there is sufficient grounds for proceeding against the accused. Where there is an express legal bar engrafted in any of the provisions of the Code or the concerned Act (under which a criminal proceeding is instituted) to the institution and continuance of the proceedings and/or where there is a specific provisions in the Code or the concerned Act, providing efficacious redress for the grievance of the aggrieved party. Where a criminal proceeding is manifestly attended with mala fide and/or where the proceeding is maliciously instituted with an ulterior motive for wreaking vengeance on the accused and with a view to spite him due to private and personal grudge. 9. Looking to the totality of facts and in view of the decisions of this Court in the case of R.A. Goenka (supra), the decisions of the Kerala High Court and the unreported decision passed in Cri. Misc. Application No.5605/2001 dt. 12.10.2007, as referred to herein above, I am of the opinion that permitting trial qua the petitioner would amount to miscarriage of justice. Hence, the criminal case and the subsequent proceedings are required to be quashed and set aside. 10. For the foregoing reasons, the petition is allowed. The criminal complaint being numbered as Criminal Case No.1697/2002 pending before the Court of learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Kutch-Bhuj and the subsequent proceedings initiated thereto are quashed and set aside only qua the petitioner herein. The petition stands disposed of accordingly. Rule is made absolute. [K.S. JHAVERI, J.] Pravin/* Top
