FIR No. 486/14 Page 1 of 10 BRIEF FACTS AND REASONS FOR THE DECISION:- 1 Case of the prosecution is that on 13.11.2014 at about 9.50 am, the complainant was going towards out patient departments of hospital. While, passing through the corridor, in new surgical block area an unknown public person came from the front and attacked her. That person pulled her hair and threw her on the floor. He hit her on her head with his hand. Thereafter, he was overpowered by guards on duty, in front of new surgical block. The accused was also shouting that "ye vo doctor hai jisne mera operation nahi kiya". Accused was then taken into police custody. During the course of investigation, he disclosed his name as Ramesh S/o Fakir Dass, R/o Uttaranchal. He was receiving treatment in the hospital and was a follow up case in surgery. Accused has been charged for the offence punishable u/s 341 IPC which has been explained u/s 339 IPC i.e Wrongful restraint- Whoever voluntarily obstructs any person so as to prevent that person from proceeding in any direction in which that person has a right to proceed, is said wrongfully to restrain that person. Exception- The obstruction of a private way over land or water which a person in good faith believes himself to have a lawful right to obstruct, is not an offence within the meaning of his section. Accused is also charged for the offence punishable u/s 323 IPC which has been explained u/s 321 IPC i.e. Voluntarily causing hurt- Whoever does any act with the intention of thereby causing hurt to any person, or with the knowledge that he is likely thereby to cause hurt to any FIR No. 486/14 Page 8 of 10 person, and does thereby cause hurt to any person, is said "voluntarily to cause hurt". Alongwith both these offences, accused is also charged for the offence punishable as well as explained u/s 354 IPC i.e. Assault or criminal force to woman with intent to outrage her modesty- Whoever assaults or uses criminal force to any woman, intending to outrage or knowing it to be likely that he will thereby outrage her modesty, [shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which shall not be less than one year but which may extend to file years, and shall also be liable to fine.] Complainant in her evidence as well as in her statement u/s 164 Cr.P.C has submitted that accused had pulled her by grabbing her hair and hit on her head once. Complainant has identified the accused person in the court. It is also submitted by the complainant that accused was taking treatment from the hospital and perturbed due to the behaviour of some doctor. During the course of her cross examination, it has been clarified by her that accused has neither used filthy language nor touched her. PW6 has also reiterated in his statement u/s 161 Cr.P.C as well as the deposition recorded before the court that he had seen the accused pulled hair of the complainant and threw her on the floor. PW8 Dr. G.S Meena had also submitted that he had also seen the accused pulling the hair of the complainant and threw her on the floor. PW9 Dr. S.V Singh had also stated in statement u/s 161 Cr.P.C as well as during the course of his examination in chief that he had seen the accused present in the court caught hold of the hair of the complainant. FIR No. 486/14 Page 9 of 10 All the witnesses in their testimonies has submitted the same facts as are mentioned by them in their statement u/s 161 Cr.P.C with respect to getting hold of the hair of the complainant and throwing her on the floor. Though, none of the witness has expressed even a single line qua any incident qua the ingredients of offence punishable u/s 354 IPC. Thus, prosecution has miserably failed to prove any of the ingredients of the offence punishable u/s 354 IPC. Complainant has herself in her cross examination clearly submitted that accused has not touched her or used any filthy language against her. In these circumstances, accused is acquitted of the offence u/s 354 IPC. Presence of accused has never been disputed at the place of the incident. The testimonies of witnesses is clear and unambiguous and stood the test of cross examination. Testimony of complainant is corroborated with the testimony of PW2, PW6 & PW8 & PW9. It is established by the testimonies of all the witnesses that accused had obstructed the way of the complainant, thereby, preventing her from proceeding and intentionally hurt her by pulling her hair and after throwing on the ground beat her. Hence, in given facts and circumstances, prosecution has proved the offence u/s 341 IPC and 323 IPC beyond reasonable doubt. Accused is thus convicted for the offence u/s 341 IPC and 323 IPC .
