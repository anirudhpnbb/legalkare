BRIEF FACTS: 2. The case of the respondent no.2­complainant in her complaint u/s 499/500 IPC is that on 15.07.2011 after the conclusion of proceedings of the day in case FIR No.145/07, PS­ R.K Puram titled as 'Harjeet Singh Vs. Gurjeet Singh' at 4:50 PM, the respondent no.2­complainant and the revisionists­accused came out of the court room. Maninder Kaur, the sister of respondent no.2­complainant, one Sh. Suraj Chauhan, Sh. H.K Moonga, Advocate, Sh. Gurjeet Singh, brother of respondent no.2­complainant and in presence of several other persons. The specific derogatory remarks have been mentioned in the complaint case. The allegation of respondent no.2­complainant is that these remarks were made by the revisionists­accused to lower the reputation of the respondent no.2­complainant in the eyes of public. 3. The revisionists­accused were summoned after recording of the pre­summoning evidence. The revision against the summoning CR No.8351/16 13.04.2017 Page no. 2/9 order was dismissed vide order dated 20.02.2016 pronounced by Ld. Special Judge, PC Act, CBI­01 (South), Saket Courts, New Delhi. The petition u/s 482 Cr.PC was then filed by the revisionists­accused in Honb'le High Court of Delhi which is pending. After appearance in the Court, notice u/s 251 Cr.PC was framed on 28.05.2016 to which both the revisionists­accused pleaded not guilty and claimed trial. The revisionists­accused are aggrieved with the order of framing of notice for the offences u/s 500 / 34 IPC against them. GROUNDS TAKEN IN REVISION PETITION: 4. The revisionists­accused have challenged the impugned order on various grounds. It is submitted that the notice was framed by Ld. Trial Court against them without giving an opportunity to be heard on the point of framing the notice. It was not appreciated by Ld. Trial Court that apart from herself, respondent no.2 examined only her elder brother Sh. Gurjeet Singh in her pre­ summoning evidence. It is alleged that Sh. Gurjeet Singh is an interested witness as he is co­accused alongwith respondent no.2 in FIR no.145/07, PS­R.K Puram and, therefore, he could not have been relied upon. No independent witness was examined by complainant. It is further averred that Ld. Trial Court did not appreciate the material evidence available on record at the time of framing of notice u/s 251 Cr.PC that would show that respondent no.2 had not approached the Court with clean hands. CR No.8351/16 13.04.2017 Page no. 3/9 The Trial Court did not give an opportunity to the revisionist to cross­examine the witnesses before the pre­charge evidence. It is also averred that the order dated 15.07.2011 passed by Ld. At the outset, it is noted that the instant complaint case is a summons trial case and does not require recording of pre­charge evidence as contended by revisionist no.1. It has also been argued by revisionists­accused that the impugned order shows that their contentions were not heard. They have, however, argued their case at length in the instant revision petition before this Court. I have considered all their contentions. 11. Section 499 IPC defines defamation as­ '499. Defamation­Whoever, by words either spoken or intended to be read, or by signs or by visible representations, makes or publishes any imputation concerning any person intending to harm, or knowing or having reason to believe that such imputation will harm, the reputation of CR No.8351/16 13.04.2017 Page no. 6/9 such person, is said, except in the cases hereinafter excepted, to defame that person'. 12. Explanation 4 of Section 499 IPC reads as follows: 'Explanation 4­ No imputation is said to harm a person's reputation, unless that imputation directly or indirectly, in the estimation of others, lowers the moral or intellectual character of that person, or lowers the character of that person in respect of his caste or of his calling, or lowers the credit of that person, or causes it to be believed that the body of that person is in a loathsome state, or in a state generally considered as disgraceful'. The accused, however, can escape liability u/s 499 r/w section 500 IPC, if his case falls within any of the 10 exceptions to section 499 IPC. 13. The issue that while uttering the alleged defamatory words against the respondent­complainant, was there an intention to harm the reputation of respondent­complainant or was there a reason to believe that the imputation made would harm the reputation of the respondent­complainant is a matter of trial. The circumstances of the incident would also have to be evaluated on the touch stone of trial to ascertain whether or not the alleged defamatory words were uttered as merely emotional and angry outburst. Further, the issue whether the alleged imputation has lowered the moral or intellectual character of the respondent­ CR No.8351/16 13.04.2017 Page no. 7/9 complainant in the estimation of the others also requires trial. No doubt, at the pre summoning stage, the respondent­complainant examined only her brother Sh. Gurjeet Singh as a witness, the Court is convinced that respondent no.2­complainant cannot be denied the opportunity to prove her case conclusively so as to explain the circumstances that would bring the alleged utterances within the four corners of the definition of defamation u/s 499 IPC. Moreso when the notice has already been framed and plea of revisionist­accused already recorded. 14. It is important to note that respondent no.2­complainant is the sister of revisionist no.2 Sh. Harjeet Singh Mendiratta and sister­ in­law of revisionist no.1 (wife of revisionist no.2). CW2 Sh. Gurjeet Singh is the elder brother of both respondent no.2­ complainant and revisionist no.2­accused. Revisionist no.1 is the wife of revisionist no.2. A bitter family dispute constitutes the bedrock of multiple litigations going on between the parties. In this background, the allegations of the respondent­complainant and defense of the revisionists require proper trial. 15. The contention of the respondent no.2­complainant is also that even though the presence of revisionist no.1­accused Kalpana was not marked in the proceeding sheets of case FIR no.145/07, PS­R.K Puram in Patiala House Courts, she was present there at that time. On the other hand, it has been contended by the revisionists­accused that revisionist no.1­accused was not even CR No.8351/16 13.04.2017 Page no. 8/9 present in the concerned Court in Patiala House Courts on that day. These contentions are a matter of trial. 16. In view of above discussion, the Court does not find any illegality or infirmity in the impugned order dated 28.05.2016 passed by the Ld. Trial Court vide which Notice u/s 251 Cr.PC for the offence u/s 499 r/w section 500 IPC was framed against the revisionists­accused. Revision petition is, therefore, dismissed. 17.
