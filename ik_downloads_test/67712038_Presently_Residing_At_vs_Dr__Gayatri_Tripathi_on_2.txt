The instant Criminal Revision is directed against order dated 16.10.2015 passed by Ld. MM-08 (West), Delhi in CC No. 323/1 (in short, 'the impugned order'), vide which Ld. Trial Court dismissed the application under Section 156(3) Cr.P.C. for registration of an FIR against the respondents for offences punishable under Section 420 / 406 / 409 / 448 / 354B / 380 / 506 / 452 / 467 / 468 / 471 / 120-B / 34 / 217 / 218 / 166A / 499 / 500 IPC. 2. The version of the revisionist in her complaint, so far as it is relevant for adjudication of the instant revision petition in nutshell is that Dr. Gayatri Tripathi ( in short 'R-1') represented herself to be the co-owner of the property bearing B-29/5, Double CR­8/16 Page 2 of 9 Storey Quarter, Ramesh Nagar, New Delhi ( hereinafter referred as "suit property") and agreed to sell the same to the complainant / revisionist for a sum of Rs.80 lacs. The petitioner paid earnest money of Rs.8 lacs i.e. Rs.7 lac by way of cheque and Rs.1 lac in cash, which was acknowledged by R-1 vide agreement / Bayana receipt on 12.08.2013. R-1 further assured the complainant that she would get NOC from other co-owner of the suit property. It is further submitted that complainant has always been ready and willing to perform her part of the contract but R-1 always avoided to execute the sale deed in favour of the complainant. 3. At request of R-1, the total sale consideration was raised from Rs.80 lac to Rs.82 lac. On 22.09.2014, Rs.30 lac was further paid in cash to R-1 and R-1 delivered vacant peaceful possession of the suit property to the complainant, who spent further amount of Rs.10 lac for carrying out renovation and repairs in the suit property. R-1 after having received substantial amount of sale consideration turned dishonest and has avoided to execute sale deed. R-1 is well known to Dr. Aditya Arya, Special Police Commissioner, who managed to register a false FIR No. 791/14 dated 16.11.2014 under Section 380 / 448 / 506 IPC PS Kirti Nagar against the complainant to dispossess the complainant from the suit property. The complainant and her son were assaulted and thrown out by SI posted at PS Kirti Nagar but the police did not take any action despite her written complaint dated 18.11.2014 to Commissioner of Police. She has also stated that some gunda elements and police officials has tresspassed into her home and stole valuable articles and instead of registration of case against the respondents, the police in collusion with R-1 have registered a false case against the complainant on false and frivolous ground. It is further submitted that she has spent Rs.58 lac from her hard earned money to purchase and renovate the suit property and she is being deprived of the said property as well as her money by unlawful acts of the accused persons. It is also stated that SI Pratap Singh accused no.4 along with 5/6 gunda elements had come in plain clothes along with accused no. 3 & 5 and out raged modesty of the complainant, who is a helpless lady and threatened to kill her and as such, accused persons are liable to be prosecuted under Section 420 / 406 / 409 / 448 / 354B / 380 / 506 / 452 / 467 / 468 / 471 / 120-B / 34 / 217 / 218 / 166A / 499 / 500 IPC. 5. Ld. Trial Court vide the impugned order dated 16.10.2015 rejected the prayer of the complainant for registration of FIR and while relying on judgments in Subhkaran Luharuka & Anr. vs. State (Government of NCT of Delhi) & Anr., ILR (2010) IV Delhi 495; M/s. Skipper Beverages Pvt. Ltd. vs. State 2002 Crl. NOC 333 (Delhi) and CR­8/16 Page 4 of 9 Ravindra Kumar vs. State ( Govt of NCT of Delhi) & Anr., 2012 VIII AD ( Delhi) 403 observed that : - "There is no fact / evidence which is to be collected by the prosecution and as such there is no requirement for investigation. No doubt Section 156 (3) Cr.P.C. empower the Magistrate to send the matters for investigation but each case has to be judged on its own merits and discretion given to the court is to be exercised in a judicial manner and not in a mechanical manner. The complainant is having control over the evidence as the same is available with him". 6. As per action taken report, on 28.10.2014 a complaint was received from R-1 wherein it was alleged that complainant with her associates had committed tresspass and theft of valuable articles in the suit property. It was also stated that R-1 is joint owner of the suit property and she had agreed to sell the same to the complainant. However, complainant did not comply with the terms and conditions of the sale agreement leaving R-1 with no option but to terminate the agreement vide legal notice dated 17.10.2014. On Complaint of R-1, an FIR No.791/14 dated 16.11.2014 under Section 448 / 380 / 506 / 34 IPC was registered at PS Kirti Nagar against the complainant. Further, it was stated that during the course of investigation, complainant did not join the investigation and could not produce any document qua her possession of the suit property and documents qua payment of Rs.30 lacs to R-1. It is also stated that complainant filed the instant complaint only as a counter attack being accused in CR­8/16 Page 5 of 9 FIR No. 791/14 under Section 448 / 380 / 506 / 34 IPC PS Kirti Nagar. 7. Ld. counsel for the revisionist has contended that respondent no.3 to 5 being influential and to help R-1 committed tresspass and theft of valuable articles, also assaulted the complainant. It is also submitted that they are helping R-1 who despite having agreed to sell the suit property and despite having received Rs.48 lacs from the complainant has turned dishonest and has not executed sale deed and on the contrary has registered a false FIR against the complainant. It is also stated that learned trial Court did not apply judicial mind and has not appreciated the facts completely and also ignored the judgment in Lalita Kumari v. Govt of UP & Ors , IX (2013) SLT 1 and Radha v. State, 179 (2011) DLT 810. 8. On the other hand, Ld. Addl. Public Prosecutor for the State has justified the order of Ld. Trial Court saying that complainant and R-1 are having property dispute. Both have alleged tresspass in the suit property against each other. It is also stated that in view of status report, registration of FIR was not required as the instant complaint is only filed to counter the FIR registered against the complainant. It is also stated that as per case of R-1, complainant herself did not perform her part of contract and avoided execution of sale deed. It is also stated that it is property dispute where complainant can seek specific performance of the agreement to sell which is a civil remedy CR­8/16 Page 6 of 9 and therefore, registration of FIR is not required. 9. It is relevant to note that each side has alleged that the other side has committed tresspass and has committed theft of valuable articles in the suit property. Each side has alleged that other side has not honoured agreement to sell. The complainant alleged that she has performed her part of the contract and yet R-1 did not execute the sale deed. R-1 has alleged that she had been ready to execute sale deed but complainant did not come forward to make balance payment necessary for execution of sale deed. 10. I agree with submissions of Ld. Addl. PP for the State that the allegations and counter allegations emanate from a property dispute and parties have a civil remedy. I also agree with the observation of learned MM that complainant is aware of all the facts and is in possession of entire evidence and there is no requirement for police investigation. If any need were to be noticed for such investigation in any specific area of dispute, the Ld. Magistrate can always resort to power available u/s 202 of the Cr.P.C. at a proper stage. 11. Ld. Counsel for the revisionist has laid much emphasis on the judgment of Hon'ble Apex Court in case titled Lalita Kumari vs. Government of Uttar Pradesh , (2014) 2 SCC 1, wherein the issue that arose for consideration before the Hon'ble Apex Court in the said CR­8/16 Page 7 of 9 judgment was whether the police officer is bound to register an FIR on receiving information relating to commission of cognizable offence under Section 154 of Cr.P.C. or police officer has power to conduct a preliminary inquiry in order to test veracity of such information before registering the same. 12. The judgment cited above is of no help to revisionist in as much as the judgment pertain to provisions relating to disclosing information to the police and their power to investigate cognizable offences. This judgment does not deal specifically with scope and ambit of powers of a Magistrate u/s 156(3) Cr.P.C. in the light of provisions contained in Chapter XII & Chapter XV of the Code. In Subhkaran Luharuka (supra), the Hon'ble Delhi High Court specifically dealt with the question how and when powers under Section 156(3) Cr.P.C. are to be exercised by a Magistrate and after discussing a number of Apex Court judgments on the said question, issued guidelines (in para 52A of the judgment reproduced above) for subordinate judicial officers "to prevent parties from misusing the process of law ( Section 156(3) CrPC)" (para 93 of the judgment). 13. Hon'ble Apex Court in Kishan Lal vs. Dharmendra Batra & Anr. , 2009 (9) SCALE 768 ruled that the revisional Court should not ordinarily interfere with the discretionary jurisdiction exercised by a Magistrate unless a jurisdictional or legal error of law is noticed. CR­8/16 Page 8 of 9 14. In Indian Oil Corpn. vs. NEPC India Ltd. (2006) 6 SCC 736, the Hon'ble Apex Court deprecated the growing tendency in business circles to convert purely civil disputes into criminal cases on account of a prevalent impression that civil law remedies are time consuming. 15. In view of the facts and circumstances of the case, the view taken by the Ld. MM-07 (West) in the impugned order dated 16.10.2015 does not suffer from any legal error and is in conformity with the guidelines laid down in the judgments of the Superior Courts as discussed above and cognizance of the offence has been taken under Section 200 Cr.P.C. for redressal of the complainant's grievances. The revision petition CR No. 8/16 is thus dismissed. Trial Court Record be sent back along with copy of this order.
