Vide this judgment, I shall dispose of the present revision petition under Section 399 r/w 397 Cr.P.C . moved on behalf of Mr. Sandeep Kumar against the order dated 19.10.2019 of Ld. MM-03, South District in Ct. Case No.9992/2019 titled as " Sandeep Kumar Vs. Rajesh Singh " whereby his complaint u/s 200 Cr.P.C. against the respondent for the offence u/s 500 IPC was dismissed. At the outset, it is to be noted that the revisionist expired during the pendency of revision petition and his wife Ms. Neha was allowed to pursue the matter on an application moved by her vide order dated 29.03.2022 of Ld. Predecessor. 2. Brief facts as per record are as under:- The revisionist had filed a complaint u/s 200 Cr.P.C. against the respondent Rajesh Singh praying for his summoning and punishing him for committing the offence u/s 500 IPC. The complaint was so filed on 30.04.2019. It was alleged that the revisionist was a decorated ex-service men from Indian Air Force and was employed as a Govt. servant in the office of Additional Deputy Comptroller & Auditor General as Assistant Audit Officer in New Delhi at that time. He was having great repute in society both at New Delhi and Bijnor, U.P. It was mentioned therein that he was having immovable property at New Delhi as well as at Bijnor, U.P. It was also mentioned that he was having immense respect among his ex- colleagues from Indian Air Force, few of them were his neighbour at Duggal Colony, Devli Road, New Delhi. It was alleged that on 16.11.2018, the respondent has filed a false complaint u/s 156(3) Cr.P.C. bearing no. 667/2018 before the Court of Ld. ACJM, Bijnor, U.P. wherein it was falsely stated that he (revisionist) had performed certain criminal acts against the respondent on 14.11.2018 at around 06:00 pm. It was stated that the relief u/s 156(3) Cr.P.C. was denied by Ld. Court. It was alleged that on getting knowledge that false case, he approached his office to prove his innocent and a certificate of attendance was issued by his office on 29.11.2018 showing that he Cr.Rev. 438/2019 Sandeep Kumar Vs. Rajesh Singh Page 2/9 was in the office on 14.11.2018 from 08:59 am till 05:32 pm. It was stated that by no means of transport, he could have been present at Bijnor on that day at 06:00 pm as alleged. It was stated that certificate so issued by his office was sufficient to show the falsity of allegations leveled against him by the respondent. It was alleged that by the act of respondent in moving a false complaint against him, he was defamed in the eyes of his colleagues at his office as they came to know about that case when he applied for the certificate of attendance. It was also alleged that the defamatory information traveled to his neighbourhood also. It was submitted that he has sent a legal notice dated 05.03.2019 to the respondent through his counsel on 07.03.2019 in this regard which was not replied to by him. Prayer was made to punish the respondent for committing the offence u/s 500 IPC. 3. The complaint was taken up for hearing by Ld. Magistrate on 02.05.2019. Cognizance was taken on 17.09.2019. The revisionist examined himself as a witness to prove his case. After considering the arguments alongwith material on record, Ld. Magistrate dismissed the complaint after holding that no ground was made out to summon the respondent as an accused vide order dated 19.10.2019. That order is being challenged in these proceedings. 4. Arguments heard. It has been argued by Ld. Counsel for revisionist that Ld. Trial Court has passed the impugned order without properly appreciating the facts alongwith material on record. It was submitted that main ground taken by Ld. Trial Court to dismiss his complaint is that the revisionist has not examined any other witness to show that his reputation was injured in the eyes of other persons. It was submitted that this was a case where the offence of defamation has been committed in writing by way of application/complaint u/s 156(3) Cr.P.C. filed before Ld. Court concerned at Bijnor so there was no requirement of examining any other witness to prove the same. It was Cr.Rev. 438/2019 Sandeep Kumar Vs. Rajesh Singh Page 3/9 argued that the revisionist was defamed in the eyes of Ld. Court where the case was filed by respondent, in the eyes of his colleagues who came to know about said case when he applied for Certificate of Attendance from his office and in the eyes of his neighbours who also got to know about it. It was submitted that the offence of defamation was prima facie made out from material on record including in view of Certificate of Attendance dated 29.11.2018 issued by his office. He has relied upon the judgment of Hon'ble Calcutta High Court in Baidya Nath Singh Vs. Muspratt and Ors. (1887) ILR 14 Cal 141 and stated that in case, Ld. Magistrate was not satisfied enough to summon the respondent then he should have directed inquiry/investigation u/s 202 Cr.P.C. He has prayed that the impugned order may be set aside. He has relied upon following judgments:- ( i) Ram Jethmalani Vs. Subramaniam Swamy AIR 2006 Delhi 300. (ii) Rajeev Bajaj Vs. Malkiat Kaur And Anr. CRM No. M-7346/2013. 5. No arguments were adduced on behalf of respondent. Record reveals that he had appeared alongwith his counsel on 25.04.2023 and adjournment was sought for adducing arguments, however none has appeared today on his behalf. It appears that respondent is not interested in participating in these proceedings. 6. I have considered the submissions made on behalf of revisionist alongwith record. Relevant portion of the impugned order is being reproduced below for ready reference:- "In the present complaint, no other witness except the complainant was examined. There is no material on record showing that reputation of complainant was injured in the eyes of any other persons or imputation of involvement in criminal Cr.Rev. 438/2019 Sandeep Kumar Vs. Rajesh Singh Page 4/9 case, lowered the character of complainant in respect or his caste of his calling, or lowered the credit of the complainant." 7. The crux of case of revisionist is that a complaint/application u/s 156(3) Cr.P.C. was moved by respondent before the Court of Ld. ACJM, Bijnor wherein it was falsely alleged that on 14.11.2018, at around 06:00 pm, he was harvesting the wheat crop from the fields of respondent in clandestine manner and that on being stopped by respondent, he alongwith other accused persons gave beatings to him. As per revisionist, he was not even present in Bijnor at the relevant date and time. He has relied upon the Certificate of Attendance issued from the office of his employer as per which his attendance in biometric attendance machine was marked on 14.11.2018 at 08:59 am and at 05:32 pm. As per revisionist, he has been defamed in the eyes of his colleagues and neighbours due to said false complaint. 8. As mentioned earlier, the revisionist had examined himself as the only witness in his pre-summoning evidence. His testimony is being reproduced below for proper appreciation:- "Alleged Rajesh Singh had filed a false complaint against me u/s 156(3) Cr.P.C. at ACJM, Bijnor, UP in the case no.667/2018. the certified copy of the complaint filed by alleged is Ex.CW1/D. He had alleged in the complaint that I had performed the offences u/s. 379 / 427 / 389 / 323 / 504 IPC at Bijnor on 14.11.2018 at around 6 PM. That after knowing the false complaint against me, I requested my office to issue my attendance certificate from office record. My office issued the certificate in this regard by which I was present in my office on 14.11.2018 from 08.59 AM till 17.32 PM. The same is Ex.CW1/A. The above mentioned case has been dismissed by the Court on 01.08.2019, the certified copy of the order dated 01.08.2019 is Ex.CW1/B. Previously on 25.03.2019 the court had already dismissed Cr.Rev. 438/2019 Sandeep Kumar Vs. Rajesh Singh Page 5/9 the application u/s 156(3) CrPC filed by the alleged, the certified copy of order dt. 25.03.2019 is Ex.CW1/C. I had served in Indian Airforce for 18 years. After that, currently I am working in CAG, India, as Asstt. Audit Officer. I do not have any case pending againt till date. Whereas the above mentioned case was falsely. My colleagues who had served in Indian Airforce are living in the same society, they also came to know about the above-montioned false case which had caused defamation to my fame which I had earned by serving for more than 20 years now. I pray from this court to summon the alleged for the offence u/s 500 IPC." 9. Section 500 IPC provides punishment for the offence of defamation which has been defined u/s 499 IPC as under:- "499. Defamation.- Whoever, by words either spoken or intended to be read, or by signs or by visible representations, makes or publishes any imputation concerning any person intending to harm, or knowing or having reason to believe that such imputation will harm, the reputation of such person, is said, except in the cases hereinafter expected, to defame that person." 10. Explanation 4 thereof is material in these facts which provides as under:- "Explanation 4.- No imputation is said to harm a person's reputation, unless that imputation directly or indirectly, in the estimation of others, lowers the moral or intellectual character of that person, or lowers the character of that person in respect of his caste or of his calling, or lowers the credit of that person, or causes it to be believed that the body of that person is in a loathsome state, or in a state generally considered as disgraceful. " Cr.Rev. 438/2019 Sandeep Kumar Vs. Rajesh Singh Page 6/9 11. So, for prima facie making out a case for the offence of defamation, it is to be shown by a person that his moral or intellectual character or credit of that person has been lowered in the estimation of others. For showing that, the revisionist has not examined any other witness in his pre-summoning evidence. It appears that examination of another witness was material for proving his case as the publication of alleged defamatory content in this matter was in the form of application u/s 156(3) Cr.P.C. filed before Ld. Court concerned at Bijnor. It appears that said application remained within the four corners of the Ld. Court as same was dismissed vide order dated 25.03.2019 and cognizance was taken on that under Section 200 Cr.P.C. Even the complaint u/s 200 Cr.P.C. was dismissed by Ld. ACJM u/s 203 Cr.P.C. due to non appearance of respondent vide order dated 01.08.2019. So, said complaint was not communicated to the colleagues or neighbours of the revisionist by the Court or the respondent. 12. It has been argued by Ld. Counsel for revisionist that his colleagues got to know about the false application against him when he applied for Certificate of Attendance from his office to prove his innocence, however said submission is not substantiated from record. It is not clear as to in which manner the request for Certificate of Attendance was made by the revisionist i.e. whether same was made in writing or orally. In case, same was made in writing, the copy of said request should have been placed on record to show as to what exactly was mentioned as the reason for making that request. In case, the request was made orally, it should have come on record as to whom the request was made and in which manner. In the absence of these specific details and in the absence of any witness to depose about that, it cannot be presumed that said false application actually came to the knowledge of his colleagues and that his reputation was harmed due to that. Same can be said about alleged defamation of the revisionist in the eyes of his neighbours as nothing has come on record to support that too. Cr.Rev. 438/2019 Sandeep Kumar Vs. Rajesh Singh Page 7/9 13. It is also to be noted that the revisionist in his testimony dated 17.09.2019 has stated only one line regarding the manner in which his reputation has been harmed, in following words:- "My colleagues who had served in Indian Airforce are living in the same society, they also came to know about the above-montioned false case which had caused defamation to my fame which I had earned by serving for more than 20 years now." 14. It is clear from above that the revisionist has completely failed to show as to in which manner defamation was caused to his fame as he has simply deposed that his colleagues also came to know about the case, however the manner in which same came to their knowledge, has not been specified. In these facts, this Court is of the view that the material on record was not sufficient to summon the respondent as an accused for the offence u/s 500 IPC. The judgments being relied upon by Ld. Counsel are distinguishable on facts. 15. It is settled law that while exercising the power of revisional jurisdiction, this Court can interefere only when the impugned order is perverse or untenable in law. Reliance is placed upon judgment of Hon'ble Apex Court in Sanjaysinh Ramrao Chavan vs. Dattatray Gulabrao Phalke and others , 2015 (3) SCC 123 wherein it has been observed as under : "14.....Unless the order passed by the Magistrate is perverse or the view taken by the court is wholly unreasonable or there is non-consideration of any relevant material or there is palpable misreading of records, the Revisional Court is not justified in setting aside the order, merely because another view is possible. The Revisional Court is not meant to act as an appellate court.The whole purpose of the revisional jurisdiction is to preserve the power in the court to do justice in accordance with the principles of criminal jurisprudence. The revisional power of the court under Sections 397 to 401 CrPC is not to be equated with that of an appeal. Unless the finding of the court, whose decision is sought to be revised, is shown to be perverse Cr.Rev. 438/2019 Sandeep Kumar Vs. Rajesh Singh Page 8/9 or untenable in law or is grossly erroneous or glaringly unreasonable or where the decision is based on no material or where the material facts are wholly ignored or where the judicial discretion is exercised arbitrarily or capriciously, the courts may not interfere with decision in exercise of their revisional jurisdiction." 14. In the above case also conviction of the accused was recorded, the High Court set aside the order of conviction by substituting its own view. This Court set aside the High Court's order holding that the High Court exceeded its jurisdiction in substituting its views and that too without any legal basis." 16. Considering the above, this Court is of the view that Ld. Magistrate has passed the impugned order after considering all the relevant factors and there is no illegality so as to justify interference by this Court in revisional jurisdiction. Accordingly, the revision petition stands dismissed. Announced in the open (Sunil Gupta) Court on 31.05.2023.
