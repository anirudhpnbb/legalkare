ORDER This petition under Section 482 of Cr.P.C. has been filed seeking quashement of the proceedings of RCT No.6178/2014 pending before JMFC, Bhopal for the offence punishable under Section 500 of IPC. 2. The facts detailed in the body of petition reveal that one Sunita Singh sister of applicant No.2 is married to Shailesh Singh, who is the respondent herein. It is alleged in the petition that after death of the father of the applicant No.2 on 16/04/1967, applicant No.2 and his step brother Jai Prakash Singh along with their mother Smt. Swarasati Singh entered into a memorandum of partition dated 27/11/2006. In terms of the memorandum, Mr. Jai Prakash Singh was entitled for 50 % share in the ancestral property and Smt. Swarasati Singh and applicant No.2 were entitled for 20% share each. The respondents approached the Deputy Commissioner of Police, East Gurgaon, Haryana and also approached the Court and on receipt of a complaint in terms of Section 156(3) of Cr.P.C. from the Court on 28/08/2013, the FIR was lodged vide Annexure A/15. 3. It is contended by the counsel that lodging of FIR against applicant No.1 was made a device by the respondent to file a complaint against the present applicant under Section 200 of Cr.P.C. and it was alleged in the complaint which is contained in Annexure P/1 that by lodging the FIR, present applicants committed an offence under Section 499 of IPC which was punishable under Section 500 of IPC. It is contended by the counsel that lodging of FIR, by the applicants does not come within the ambit of Section 499 of IPC inasmuch as, exception 8 thereof, specifically provides that accusation against any person who has lawful authority, cannot be treated as defamation. It is contended by the counsel that when the accusation is made in good faith to a lawful authority, the same cannot be brought within the fourcorners of the definition of "defamation" as provided under Section 499 of IPC. Learned counsel has also placed reliance on illustration which is inserted just below the 8th exception to Section 499 of IPC. It is further contended by the counsel that as per the definition of defamation, there has to be publication of the imputation which has been made with an intention to cause harm to the reputation of a person and the imputation should also lower the moral and intellectual character of a person. 4. In paragraph 14 it is clearly stated that the copy of the complaint as well as Signature Not Verified Signed by: ASTHA SEN Signing time: 28-09- 2024 14:24:07 NEUTRAL CITATION NO. 2024:MPHC-JBP:49179 7 MCRC-42685-2018 notices were delivered to the office of the present respondent at Bhopal and the said notices/complainant travelled to various clerical desks, therefore, the esteem of complainant was lowered down and he was defamed at the behest of present applicants. 10. It is thus clear from the averments made in paragraphs 11, 12 and 14 that the complainant is aggrieved by the detriment to his reputation not only in the State of Haryana but also in the State of M.P. So far as exception 8 to Section 499 of IPC is concerned, the lawful authority for the purposes of said section, can be treated to be the police authorities of Haryana but the authorities at Madhya Pradesh cannot be treated as lawful authority inasmuch as, the incident mentioned in the FIR (Annexure A/15) pertains to Gurgaon, Haryana only. Therefore, at this stage it cannot be said that the police authorities of the State of M.P. was lawful authority within the meaning of exception 8 to Section 499 of IPC. It is apparent that at a premature stage, this petition under Section 482 of Cr.P.C. has been filed by the applicants seeking quashment of the complaint. 11. In view if the aforesaid, in the considered view of this Court the complaint particularly paragraphs 11,12 and 14 thereof, prima facie reveals that the allegations levelled in the complaint is as regards accusation vide Annexure A/15 is not only to lawful authority at Haryana but communication thereof to the authorities at Madhya Pradesh as well. Hence, this Court is not inclined to entertain this petition. The interference at this premature stage is not warranted inasmuch as, the same would amount to conduct a mini trial at the stage of exercising the inherent power under Section 482 of CR.P.C.
