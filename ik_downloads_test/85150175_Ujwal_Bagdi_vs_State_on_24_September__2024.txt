Admittedly, the Ld. Magistrate, vide his order dated 27.09.2018, was pleased to observe that a prima facie case regarding commission of offences punishable inter alia u/s 144 / 148 / 149 IPC is made out against the revisionists herein / accused persons. Admittedly, all the said provisions of law fall under chapter VIII of the Indian Penal Code , which chapter requires a minimum of five persons to be designated as an unlawful assembly, subject to fulfilment of other conditions enumerated in the said chapter. Admittedly, the chargesheet reflects that only four persons (i.e. revisionists herein / accused persons) were chargesheeted by the police. Admittedly, the chargesheet does not reflect that any person involved in the commission of the said offences is absconding or is untraceable. Crl. Revision No: 43/2023 Ujwal Bagdi vs State page no. 6 of 8 Admittedly, the Ld. Magistrate never summoned the other persons placed in column No. 12 of the chargesheet. Apparently, the Ld. Trial Court has erred in observing that a prima facie case is made out against the present revisionists / accused persons under the said provisions of law. As such, the impugned orders dated 27.09.2018 and 28.01.2019 are liable to be set aside on this short ground alone. Given the grave nature of error, it would not be appropriate to dismiss the present revision petition on the technical ground of limitation. As such, the delay in filing of present petition is condoned. 9. The other ground for challenge, as pressed by the present revisionists / accused persons, is that the provision left behind against the present revisionists / accused persons, after the allegations punishable u/s 144 / 148 / 149 IPC are deleted, is 506/34 IPC ; which is non-cognizable in nature and the same renders the entire FIR as well as proceedings emanating therefrom to be illegal. It is argued that Police could not have investigated the matter without the permission of the Magistrate concerned. 10. This Court has considered the above argument as well. At the very outset, this Court must observe that the complaint filed by the complainant / informant u/s 156(3) Cr.P.C. r/w Section 190 Cr.P.C. at the relevant time inter alia reflects allegations regarding commission of offences falling under Chapter XVII of IPC by the present revisionists / accused persons and some others. The said allegations are not 'non-cognizable' in nature. As such, the above argument could Crl. Revision No: 43/2023 Ujwal Bagdi vs State page no. 7 of 8 not be upheld by any stretch of imagination. 11. In the entire facts and circumstances, the impugned orders dated 27.09.2018 and 28.01.2019 are hereby set aside. The Ld. Magistrate is requested to bestow his consideration in the matter afresh on the point of charge, keeping in view the facts and circumstances of this case. 12. With these observations, the present revision petition stands disposed of. 13. TCR be sent back alongwith copy of this judgment. 14. This revision file be consigned to Record Room, as per rules.
