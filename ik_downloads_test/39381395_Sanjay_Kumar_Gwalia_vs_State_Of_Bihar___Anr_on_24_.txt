No.53050 of 2013 (3) dt.24-01-2017 3/8 Katihar finding a prima facie case against the petitioner vide order dated 2.8.2013, issued processes against the petitioner for the offences under Section 500 and 504 of the Indian Penal Code. The aforesaid order issuing processes against the petitioner is under challenge by the petitioner in this application under Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure . It has been submitted on behalf of the petitioner that admittedly the petitioner was working as the District Sub Registrar and was exercising quasi judicial power at that time and even according to the complaint petition, whatever allegation has been attributed against the petitioner it happened during discharge of his official duty but the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate has taken cognizance without appreciating the fact that Section 197 of the Code of Criminal Procedure clearly provides that no court shall take cognizance of any offence alleged to have been committed by a public servant while acting or purporting to act in the discharge of his official duty, took cognizance of the offence under Section 500 and 504 of the Indian Penal Code and the processes were issued against the petitioner without the required sanction from the appropriate authority. Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.53050 of 2013 (3) dt.24-01-2017 4/8 In support of his contention, the learned counsel for the petitioner has cited a large number of decisions, however, as it is an established principle that it is not necessary to quote all the above decisions as cited by the petitioner. Further submission on behalf of the petitioner is that in this case, the learned court below has found prima facie a case under Section 500 and 504 of the Indian Penal Code and both Sections are penal section , in which a person can be punished, if there are ingredients against him as provided in Section 499 of the Indian Penal Code. However, in the present case, no case is made out under Section 500 and 504 of the Indian Penal Code against the petitioner, as from the complaint petition, it appears that first two sentences what he has uttered were against the Collector and about the power used by him, no doubt the third attribution may cause some harm to the reputation of the Opposite Party No.2 but considering the facts and circumstances of the present case, the aforesaid attribution appears to be ornamental in nature and the learned court ought to have considered this aspect of the matter especially when he has not find prime facie case under Sections 147 , 323 , 342 , 384 and 379 of the Indian Penal Code but the learned court below in a very Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.53050 of 2013 (3) dt.24-01-2017 5/8 mechanical manner has taken cognizance under Sections 500 and 506 of the Indian Penal Court without appreciating the aforesaid fact. In view of the aforesaid submission , the learned counsel for the petitioner prays for quashing of order dated 2.8.2013, by which prima facie a case has been found under Sections 500 and 504 of the Indian Penal Code and the processes were issued. Heard learned A.P.P. and the learned counsel for the Opposite Party No.2. It has been submitted on behalf of the learned counsel for the Opposite Party no.2 that the words as stated or uttered by the petitioner as mentioned in the complaint petition itself shows that the same has lower down his dignity and has been used just to humiliate the complainant and harm the reputation of the petitioner, as such the order issuing processes against the petitioner under Section 500 and 504 of the Indian Penal Code is just and proper and does not require interference by this Court. Having heard both sides . From perusal of the materials available on the record, it appears that the petitioner at the time of occurrence was functioning as the District Sub Registrar of Katihar and the complaint petition also shows that it happened Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.53050 of 2013 (3) dt.24-01-2017 6/8 during the official duty of the petitioner and so far some utterances, which have been mentioned in the complaint petition are concerned, those are mainly against the Collector as he had directed him not to proceed with execution of sale-deed and the petitioner was explaining his power. So far third utterances are concerned, the possibility of inserting the same in order to make allegation serious can not be ruled out, which will also appear from the fact that though in the complaint petition several allegations had been levelled against the petitioner for making offences against him under Sections 147 , 323 , 384 , 379 of the Indian penal Code and the court below found those allegations not true. Even if for the sake of brevity, it is accepted to be true, the petitioner was discharging his official duty, those hot words may have been uttered in course of hot exchange of word between the parties and it may not be intentional as it appears that petitioner was not happy with the direction issued by the Collector and the Opposite Party No.2 was insisting for that, as such in that background also, the order issuing processes without having sanction from the appropriate authority does not appear to be proper and against the provisions as contained in Section Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.53050 of 2013 (3) dt.24-01-2017 7/8 197 of the Code of Criminal Procedure , otherwise it will be difficult for a public servant to work in such a situation. Apart from that the utterances used by the petitioner does not disclose the ingredient as mentioned in Section 499 of the Indian Penal Code and they may be an imagination of the Opposite Party no.2 as the petitioner was not ready to accept the prayer of the Opposite party no.2 and hence the aforesaid allegation has been leveled. It is human nature that one has a tendency to exaggerate the thing in order to prove himself right and as there is hot exchange of words, so allegation as mentioned in complaint petition has been leveled, so far section 504 of the Indian Penal is concerned, there was nothing in the aforesaid utterances, which may lead to provoke the Opposite Party No. 2 to breach the peace. From the discussions as made above, the order dated 2.8.2013 issuing processes against the petitioner does not appears to be just and proper and taking cognizance against the petitioner without proper sanction by the learned court below is against the provisions of the Code of Criminal Procedure . Accordingly, this application is allowed and the impugned order dated 2.8.2013 is quashed. Patna High Court Cr.Misc. No.53050 of 2013 (3) dt.24-01-2017 8/8 (Vinod Kumar Sinha, J) chn/- U
