Arguments heard at length. Material perused. As per the provisions of Section 499 of IPC, the offence of defamation consist of three essential ingredients, viz.: CR No. 39/2015 (Bhupesh Kumar) Kalpana Mendiratta & Anr Vs State & Anr Spl.Judge (PC Act) CBI­01/20.02.2016 6 (1) making or publishing any imputation concerning any person; (2) such imputation must have been made by words either spoken or intended to be read, or by signs, or by visible representations, and (3) such imputation must have been made with the intent to harm, or with knowledge or belief that it will harm the reputation of the person concerned. Reverting to the present matter the respondent no. 2 who is complainant in the complaint case appeared in the witness box as CW1 when she has specifically stated that on 15.07.2011 in the court of Ld MM certain heated arguments were exchanged between her and both the petitioners and at about 4.45 to 4.50 PM when they came out of the court no. 208 of Ld MM, both the petitioners made derogatory remarks against her in order to lower her reputation before the persons present and general public, in presence of their elder brother Gurjeet Singh, sister Maninder Kaur, her neighbour Suraj Chauhan and her counsel. As observed by Ld Trial Court in the impugned order the respondent no. 2 has stated that the following remarks were made against her by both the accused. a) Sonia you are a cheater, forger and in the habit creating and manufacturing the forged documents, with the dishonest intention to grab the property left by the mother. b) You always live in the company of uncalled persons having low reputation and also maintaining bad relationship with them. Yaha tak ki tune police Inspector ko of set kar liya hai tere kahne ke mutabik hi court mein ata hai aur report banata hai." Sh Gurjeet Singh, another brother of the parties appeared in the witness box before Ld Trial Court as CW2 and has corroborated the statement of complainant on account of the date, place and time of the incident and the words uttered by both the petitioners. As per their statement the incident took place outside the court no. 208 Saket. Ld counsel for petitioner has submitted that false and motivated complaint has been filed by the respondent no. 2 as she is having grudge against the petitioners for registration of FIR against her. In this respect undisputedly, certain other litigations are also pending in the different courts. Here the CR No. 39/2015 (Bhupesh Kumar) Kalpana Mendiratta & Anr Vs State & Anr Spl.Judge (PC Act) CBI­01/20.02.2016 8 question arises whether it could be the ground to allow the present revision. In this regard it is found that it is well settled law of land that earlier ill-will/enmity between the parties is double edge sword which may or may not be the reason to file complaint against each other and the court is required to examine the evidence of witnesses with caution and diligence. In this matter only pre-summoning evidence has been recorded so far. The defence of the petitioners not came on Trial Court record so far. Hence, it would be too early to comment if the complaint filed by the respondent no. 2 is false and motivated or not as the accused has not yet got opportunity to cross examine the witnesses examined in support of the complaint. Hence, at this stage the present revision cannot be allowed only on the ground that some earlier litigations are pending between the parties. Further, in this matter as stated earlier the complainant in unmistakable terms has deposed that the respondents have levelled certain imputations on account of her character and her brother CW2 Gurjeet Singh had corroborated her testimony in this respect. In respect to the imputations, it is found that they are prima facie libelous and per-se defamatory touching the moral and character of the respondent no. 2. Under these circumstances, no illegality or infirmity has been found in the impugned order. The judgments relied upon by the revisionists have been perused carefully and it is found that the findings of the said judgments are not applicable to the facts of the instant case.
